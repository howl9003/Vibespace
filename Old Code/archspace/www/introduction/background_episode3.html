BACKGROUND
 
EPISODE III
 
Janska Agamar entered the hall, waiting by the doorway only long enough for the Master of Ceremonies to call out her name. Already, many of the members of the council had gathered to hear the latest proposal and vote on it. Some smiled and nodded in her direction, some glared at her and looked away, or ignored her completely. In the back of the seating, two members whispered together and didn't appear to notice she was there. The Council Leader was already in his seat, though by rights he should have come last as the most 'powerful' leader. "Probably here to prevent the sort of bickering that crashed the last council before it could get off the ground." That had been a disaster, with the arguments growing into shouting matches, and too many leaders quitting the meeting in protest to maintain the quorum needed to vote. This, the smiles, glares, and the bickering, was usual.
Agamar was the leader of one of the two political factions in the council, with Vrolf Harikson as her opponent. The two factions differed wildly over how the Council should be run. Harikson's faction believed that one domain should supply the ships for the council fleet, with the rest of the domains in the council providing the funds necessary. Unsurprisingly, the domain they wanted to be built up was one of the members of their party, and the taxes would hit them less hard then Agamar's faction, which was made up of smaller domains. The only reason that Agamar's faction had managed to block the plan was because Harikson's faction had the tendency to try to bully the other domains in the council, reducing their number of votes, as that was determined by reputation as well as size. Unsurprisingly, Agamar and her faction believed this was the first step to a full takeover of the council.
There were only two other political forces- Agamar's faction and the Council Leader. Agamar's faction believed that there should be no tax, but that each domain should contribute 10% of its standing fleet to the council fleet. The opposing faction blocked this, of course, since their larger fleets would take more damage from the battles. The Council Leader was one of only three faction-less council members. The other two, the ones in the back, were so small that they never affected the votes. The Council Leader had been elected more out of exhaustion then out of anything else. Early on, the council had polarized, with Agamar and Harikson as the two candidates. Even after months of debate, no one could gain a clear lead and gain the position. When the current Council Leader, Jacobs, joined the race, no one had thought that he had any chance. Yet, somehow he had gained the needed votes, most likely by threats and 'gifts', and he had ascended to the position of Council Leader. Now of course, he was growing rich, being courted by both factions to secure his support, and thus he was now possibly one of the most powerful warlords in truth within the Council.
Finally, though, the last of the council members filtered in, Harikson being the last. He directed an oily smile Janska's way, which she returned with the glare that had given her the nickname 'Fire Eyes'. The smile faded from Vrolf's face and looking slightly pale he found his seat, moving a step faster then usual. Satisfied with the results, Janska turned towards the podium as the Council Leader began.
"Let this meeting come to order," He said, quietly, the speakers controlled from the podium amplifying him so that everyone could hear easily. "This meeting is now in session. We are here to vote on the proposal from Council Member Harikson, concerning the idea that one empire would contribute the ships of the Council Fleet¡¦" Janska didn't need to hear anything more, and stabbed the NAY button on her council. At about the same time, everyone else also voted, and on the front of each podium, a light snapped on, red for 'Nay', green for 'Aye'. On the board behind the speaker, a tally kept track of the votes and how each side was doing. As before, the sides were about equal. Satisfied, Janska tuned back into the speech.
"¡¦And furthermore, this council fleet will be produced and maintained by the Taurus Republic." The Taurus Republic. The domain of the Council Leader. Janska knew that there was no way her faction could pay the political coin needed to bring the balance back to par. With the Council Leader behind the proposal, the weaker members of her faction would change sides. Already, the votes showed 60% 'Aye', 39% 'Nay', and 1% 'Undecided'. As the screen updated, she received three messages, from three of the more powerful members of her faction. She choose to read those first.
"Councilwoman Janska Agamar, I am sorry that I must inform you that I have decided to vote 'Aye' to this proposal. The decision is not wholly mine- if I refuse, Harikson's faction has promised to destroy me utterly, and I believe him. Recently, my domain has suffered a spate of infiltrations that I can trace to their faction, as a method of showing me the consequences if I refuse. Again, I am sorry. Councilman Ar'eey Valchesso"
Janska looked up, blinked and looked again in horror. It read '96% 'Aye', 4% 'Nay''. She knew that she was the only one who had not changed over her vote, and it didn't matter. The council was now controlled by Harikson's faction, and she knew if that her domain remained, it would only be a matter of time before she was removed as a force in the galaxy. Quickly, she sent the needed orders.
Recall all ships from the Council Fleet. Cancel all joint exercises. Put the ships in a strong defensive stance. Trust no one, not even those of our council. Interdict our space. She signaled her request to speak to the Council Leader. After a few seconds, it died, sign of a veto. She attempted again. It was vetoed again. That decided it, she knew. The Council Leader was firmly in Harikson's camp, and she would have no voice in the decisions of the council. She stood and started to leave. "Going somewhere, Councilwoman?" Harikson sneered from his place near the podium. He laughed loudly as she ignored him and left.
Days later, her shuttle touched down at the primary spaceport of her capital world, Loranis. Her ministers of Defense and Intelligence met her there with grim news.
"We have recently picked up signs of massive efforts to sabotage our ships, destroy our shipyards, labs and government buildings, and¡¦ we have found several plots to bomb a great many elementary schools. We believe these originated from Harikson's domain." The Spymaster reported miserably. No surprise on the school bombing plots- it was Harikson's style to slay the weak to force the stronger to capitulate. Her defense minister didn't have much better news.
"Council Fleet patrols have been probing our defensive sphere. Unfortunately, we show a massing of the Council Fleet, and behind them, troop transports belonging to Harikson and Jacobs." The Minister of Defense said. No surprise there, either. Harikson planned to remove all opposition to his rule of the council, starting with his greatest opponent- Janska Agamar. Suddenly, a plan formed in Janska's mind. It was risky, but all that was left to her.
"Send messages to everyone who had been affiliated with my faction. Offer them the option of joining a new Council- The Alliance for Liberty. Immediately withdraw from the Council and form a new one with that name." She quickly instructed.
Her two advisors looked at each other, then looked at her, saluted, and went at a dead run to complete her instructions. She knew that her actions, or lack of them, would lead to war- but this way, perhaps not a war without allies.
