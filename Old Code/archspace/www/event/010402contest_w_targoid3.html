The Trak'na Chronicles: SKIN-DEEP
by Carlo Jan P. Garingarao


The void, engulfing him in its cold, chilling embrace. Its black talons clawed down into his very core, turning his ethereal limbs into neutronium weights. Was this blessed Honar at last? It couldn't be. The sacred texts described it as a warm place, a world of unparalleled contentment and bliss. Or had his essence finally arrived at paradise... only to find it an eternal well of darkness?

Surely he couldn't be dead. He refused to accept that this was all that lay in store for all who died such a noble death as he and the Retribution's crew befell. The universe could not be as cruel as this. He blinked to dispel the darkness, and a dagger of pain stabbed at his head... Wait, how could one have such physical pains if he was dead?

His head... ah, his neck was stiff, and ached as he slowly swung it from side to side. His arms... heavy and leaden; he left them alone for the moment. He took a deep breath, his chest sore as it expanded to inhale air. The air reeked of organic waste, saturated, humid, but he was too haggard to care for the moment. By Jarnapah's staff, he was truly, blessedly alive, mindless of the wearied condition he was in.

After a few minutes of regaining sensation to his entire body, he discovered a sprained arm from the pain in attempting to move it, a possibly-abraded left cheek from the stinging sensation... as well as immovable bonds clamped around his wrists. He was in a reclined position, tilting about 30 degrees, chained against a soft, damp wall that seemed to pulse with life. Aside from that, he had no idea where he was in the impenetrable gloom.

'Overseer Jehrom, you are finally awake,' a thought clearly tinged with relief filtered into his mind. 'We were beginning to fear that you were fatally injured as they brutally handled us from the ship. You passed out after channelling the psionic wave.'

He was not alone in the abyss, sensing Navigator Virashti to his right. 'Brother, the ship... the armada... where have they brought us? I thought we were dead for certain...'

'So did all of us,' Aryka, another one of the Retribution's navigators, joined the silent psionic communion, her thoughts tainted with fear. 'None of us knows what has happened to the ship, but the remainder of the Initiative armada managed to escape, of that I am certain. I witnessed them flee into hyper-drive just as the Targoid boarded our ship.'

Jehrom, former supreme commander of the routed Initiative armada, Bosalian delegate to the Trak'na Peace Initiative, felt the first glimmer of hope at the news. Knox had escaped, and would warn the entire star cluster of the unforeseen Targoid infestation in Trak'na. Somehow, the female Human commander must find a way to defeat the seemingly-invincible Targoid hordes; she was the Initiative's only hope.

But that did not mean that he should stop looking for a means to escape.

'How many of us are being held captive?'

Aryka, speaking as a way to keep herself calm, projected, 'Ten; nine navigators and you were chosen after they ushered us all to the hall of summons.'

'The others?'

Her brief silence was more than sufficient for a reply, Jehrom sensed her head in droop in despair. 'All slain... brutally.'

Pacifistic as he was, Jehrom could not help but feel the faintest clawing of rage in his heart. Those were his men, more than two hundred Bosalian souls under his charge; all murdered. The Overseer silently offered a brief prayer for his crew, then focused his pent-up anger into other matters. Anything to keep his mind off wreaking vengeance on their Targoid captors.

'Perhaps you should know, Overseer,' Virashti hesitantly voiced out his thoughts. 'The three of us are all the only ones left from the ten. Two hours ago, they came in and dragged four out; an hour ago, three more of our kin.'

Aryka's psionic cry followed, so clearly close to hysteria, 'Why are we being kept alive? What horrors will befall us? Jarnapah, 'tis far worse than death!'

Jehrom refused her contagious panic to reach him, and shut himself out from the two. It was useless to attempt any mental assaults as for the moment; the psionic wave pulse they had recently created had completely drained them all. Even maintaining mental conversation was beginning to strain them in their weakened state.

The only thing they could do was wait... wait for their strength to return or for the Targoid to arrive, whichever came first.

"Order, please!" Cosette Knox raised her voice as high as she could, bracing both hands on the console before her. "We have little time to plan. The Targoid could be heading for Central even as we speak."

The entire Initiative assembly, or what was left of it, quieted down. The remnants of the once-formidable Initiative armada had arrived at Central an hour ago, barely a quarter of the ships had survived from the Targoid ambush at Ontonus IV. In panic, or at the loss of Teradin Garpenal, the Xerusian fleet had headed straight for Dominion space, and the Buckaneer corsairs had vanished after their retreat.

"Our first course of action is to gather up another armada, this time for the defense of the member councils should the Targoid decide to strike," she announced.

"And who appointed you as the new Overseer now?" an Equinus boomed from the assembly with a challenging tone. "We should vote on the matter."

After a quick election, the results were 22-1, in favor of Commander Cosette Knox as the new Overseer of the Initative. The Equinus uttered a barrage of incomprehensible curses, and stormed out of the convention hall.

"Now, if there are no further interruptions, I suggest you all contact your respective councils," Cosette continued. "We need all the ships we can gather as early as possible."

'But you saw what happened back at Ontonus IV,' a Bosalian projected his thoughts to the entire convention. 'We could barely target them with our weapons... they are simply too fast. How do you expect us to stop them this time?'

"To tell you the truth, I have no idea as of the moment," she allowed. "That is why I am leaving for Felran after this session. If there's a weapon capable of stopping the Targoid, the Buckaneers should know."

Kel-ten, her Tecanoid first officer, reminded her, "If you recall, Overseer, the Buckaneers did not regroup at Central as they were ordered. This may indicate that they have abandoned the Initiative altogether. Seeking aid from them may as well be futile."

"I know, but I have to try," she replied wearily. "We should appoint a commander for the armada while I am gone, which will hopefully be less than two cycles."

An hour later, Cosette's frigate departed from Central's orbit, on a direct course for the Buckaneer trade world of Feltran.

Jehrom withdrew into himself, and slowly extended his psionic feelers outward. He had recovered enough strength for small mental exertions in the previous hour; Aryka and Virashti were still too drained to use their powers.

At once, he could sense the Targoid collective all around them: a stifling, encompassing thought, an expansive web linking the multitiude of Targoid on whatever planet they were in to some unknown focus. Jehrom ignored it as best as he could, setting up a weak barrier to prevent them from detecting his psionic signature. There was no point in slipping into their collective; the overpowering thoughts would only strain him.

His range did not reach far, but he could sense no other minds within it aside from the mindlessly-obedient Targoid being directed into their endless tasks. Tainted with despair, Jehrom abandoned the search.

'Nothing,' he announced to the two. 'I felt no one else aside from them... at least within my reach. Our kin may have been brought to some distant location beyond my range.'

Aryka, her fears confirmed, replied, 'They are dead, I tell you! There is no use for all of this, no way of escape. We are being stored like mere food for these demons, and when they hunger...'

Bright, blinding light flooded into their cell, streaming from a gaping door that opened silently before them. As Jehrom blinked to dispel the spots dancing before his eyes, he could feel terror grip Aryka, and despair in Virashti's silence. He had to get them under control, or escape would truly be impossible. He faintly made out sleek, winged forms slipping into the cell and positioning to either side of them.

By now, their vision had returned: six Targoid drones, armored in effulgent, dark green carapace. Their thin, membranous wings rapidly buzzed as a pair attended to each of their chains, releasing them. Their long, razor-sharp mandibles clicked sharply in some form of communication. A Targoid took hold of each of their freed arms, and began hauling them out of the dark cell.

Jehrom tried to reach the other two; Aryka was too deep into mindless panic to reply, while Virashti replied with a faint grim vow to look for a way to flee when the chance presented itself. But the Targoid held them with vise-like grips, struggling was out of the question.

The small convoy navigated through a maze of well-illuminated, organic tunnels, until they emerged into the surface. Jehrom was awestruck at the sight; massive Targoid hives towering over them, dotting the gray, mountainous landscape on the planet they were on. In the distance, tiny, ant-like life-forms scrambled over a half-constructed hive. Overhead, a platoon of sleek ships swooped past the hive cluster in patrol.

They swiftly sped into the largest of the structures, and found themselves in an enormous cavernous space filled with countless Targoid. Some were winged like the ones carrying them, engaged in furious combat; training probably. Others were bearing capsule-like pupa, others guarding the hive's many entrances. To the far right, wide rows of their hyper-speed ships were docked.

'Jarnapah, how long have they been in Trak'na? How in the world did we not manage to foresee this?' Virashti spoke in disbelief at the organization surrounding them.

Jehrom kept silent, and they moved on. Down into another tunnel they went, descending for a minute until reaching yet another large chamber. It was dim, organic walls slick with transparent slime, and was unusually empty. Their ushers chained them into another set of restraints, then respectfully left the chamber. The only way out of the chamber was the way they came; the circular opening on the top of the dim cavern.

'Look,' Aryka surprisingly spoke, 'to the left corner...'

They spied a small pile of items; tattered heaps of cloth, some metal shards, complete Xerusian battle armor, and a large heap of golden vestments Jehrom knew too well. It was the same clothing they were wearing now.

'Jarnapah,' Virashti was panicking now as well. 'Remul, Iyari... we are all going to die!'

Jehrom was at a loss for words. Could it be true? Were they the only ones left alive in this hell? A sharp series of clicks rang from the shadows before them, and Aryka moaned in sheer terror. Slowly, as if to taunt them, it emerged from the darkness; a Targoid, larger than the others, winged, but its abdomen bloated. It floated closer, regarded them with narrow, crimson eyes, and its massive mouthparts chattered again.

Another figure emerged from the gloom, but it was a Bosalian. It staggered drunkenly towards them, and halted at the obese Targoid's right. A reflex sliver of hope laced Aryka's voice as she recognized their brother.

"Remul! You are alive!"

For an instant, Jehrom entertained the distant probability that Remul had somehow manipulated the Targoid, and managed to stay alive in hiding. But only for an instant. Something about Remul was wrong: the blank expression on his face, the numerous, random scratch marks that ran across his face, and the slack way his arms hung from his side. It was as if the Bosalian navigator was intoxicated, his empty eyes staring them without the glint of cognizance.

"Remul?" Virashti frowned at his silence, also sensing something out of place.

The Targoid chirped shrilly once more, and Remul's mental voice filtered into their minds. It was alien, foreign, somehow repulsive as their received it.

'You are about to participate in the latest pinnacle of our endless quest for evolution,' Remul began. 'Needless to say, we request your cooperation.'

'What are you talking about?' Virashti snarled in reply. 'Have you gone mad? What do you mean 'we'? Let us out of here, quickly!'

Remul turned to the Targoid, like a child seeking for guidance from a parent. 'I am sorry. I am no longer the entity you know as Remul. I am the new face of the Targoid warrior, cloaked beneath the countenance of its foes.'

A pair of long, ebony mandibles slipped out his small oral-slit, and clicked together in the familiar Targoid tongue. Aryka emitted a choked cry of horror at Remul's transformation. At once, Jehrom understood. Somehow, the Targoid had found a way to live within a host, controlling it and all its knowledge, skills, powers... everything.

It was truly a fate far worse than death. Not even Cosette Knox would foresee this.

'Your star cluster is unlike the others,' Remul, acting as mindless translator, continued. 'We must admit, it is not easy to conquer. It has no wars, no petty disputes; which makes outright invasion against your Initiative a great waste of resources. That was when we realized there were other new ways of achieving our goal.'

'By infiltrating our ranks,' Jehrom finished for him. 'by slipping into our bodies.'

Remul drawled on, ignoring the Overseer, 'By controlling your bodies, assuming your identities, we could shatter your precious Initiative... and destroy the pieces one by one. No one would ever suspect a thing. And what better entity to manipulate than you, Jehrom, Overseer of the Initiative.'

The Targoid by Remul's side tittered, and Remul took a step backward. It held itself aloft with its buzzing wings, and slowly approached Aryka. Its body arched, bloated abdomen pointed forward, and waited an inch from Aryka's ashen face as she was rooted in place by fear.

Jehrom, desperate, blurted out, 'What about the others? Iyari? Mherut? What happened to them?'

'The process is still quite new to us,' Remul calmly explained. 'Attempts to control them were... disastrous. I was the first to succeed.'

A slender, worm-like creature slipped out of the operculum at the abdomen's tip, part of still within the Targoid. It waved in the air, as if testing its new surroundings; its stiletto-like snout at its front end passing dangerously close to Aryka's face. Suddenly, it shot out of the Targoid with lightning speed, and Jehrom realized a second later that it had bored into Aryka's head through her ear. All that was left to see was a few inches of its rear end, twisting as the rest of it slipped into her brain.

Aryka's face became blank, then distorted in pain, and she thrashed in her restraints wildly. Remul advanced, and released her from her chains, and she dropped to the soft floor, clawing at her face, mindless of the wounds she gouged on herself.

Remul ignored her, and announced, 'Next.'

Jehrom knew that if he attempted to break the chains that held them, the exertion would leave him too weak to levitate out of the chamber. He needed more time... Virashti projected to him a desperate plan.

'Your plan won't work,' Jehrom interjected. 'What about the people who know me? They'll detect something wrong within a minute. And who would believe I managed to escape the Targoid ambush at Ontonus?'

Remul cocked his head, 'Too easy. Everything you are is in here, even your memories.' He pointed to his head. 'We control that as well. And who could not help but be swept in admiration at tomorrow's news: "Overseer Jehrom escapes Targoid captivity, courageously risks life to warn Initiative of threat, Galactic Hero."'

The Targoid was now positioned before Virashti, and the worm was beginning its struggle out of its parent's abdomen. Virashti cried out, and the bonds on Jehrom's wrists snapped open. A second later, Remul and the Targoid collapsed on the floor as Virashti mentally assaulted them. Jehrom shot out of the chamber as fast as he could mentally propel himself, while glancing downward at his brother one last time.

Virashti slumped downward, held only by his chains, truly spent. He had given all he had for Jehrom's escape. Remul and the Targoid implanter were writhing on the floor, dazed by the psionic assault.

Loud clicks before him; the six Targoid guards. Jehrom dispatched them quickly, but he was beginning to feel his powers starting to drain again. The Bosalian Overseer advanced through the dim tunnel, fleeing, surrounded by more than a million Targoid on a forsaken planet without a single ally.
