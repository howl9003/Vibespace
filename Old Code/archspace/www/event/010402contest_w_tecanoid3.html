Into The Depths
by Victoria Dreyer


The darkness of the void enveloped the ship as it slipped, ever slowly, from the berth that bore it into the seeming endlessness of the planet's core. Orbs of mineral deposits suspended in a strange, viscous fluid surrounded the vessel as it drew away from the docking back, intentions of exploration temporarily muted by awe.

Within; Wits focused to the point of paranoia, Techanoid crew venturing forth into the unknown. A new planet, a world and environment equally unusual, the lights from the research craft's bows the only offering of visual aid deeper than perhaps a thousand feet in the murky depths.

An ocean in a crater; An enigma wrapped in a question, enveloped in a mystery. An entire world encased in the thin rind of an orange, the pulp within liquefied to the consistency of a jelly. The outer, barren as an airless moon, devoid of life nor features, but for the scattering of craters around the planet's equator, scars of a long-past battle.

Alone, in the endlessness of space, a ball of death far from the blessing of the single star's rays. A mere third in line, perhaps, yet without the touch enough to grant life to the surface. Within, however; A world unknown, scanners, for all their technology, unable to pierce more than the uppermost layer of the glutinous underworld.

And thus, they now sought to explore the depths, in search of that elusive knowledge that the lack of sensory penetration denied of them. Even now, as they slipped deeper into the fluid, they were travelling blind; Sightless as the unborn child within the womb.

The solid thrum of the vehicles engines invaded every sense of the beings within. Amongst them, a leader of men, a being of untold intellect. Chosen for this skill, to participate in a mission of utmost importance; To uncloak the enigma, and allow the mystery to rise into the light of conversation.

The fellow's commands muted, as though the fluid about the ship had somehow penetrated the thick hull, to dull the voices of those within, as they'd speak to one another. Words cut short, and voices low, nervousness would flow through the crew as a ripple through a pond. Even the commander, for all his bravery, affected thus.

Not fear, no, he'd deny, if questioned. Apprehension, yes. Venturing into the unknown, one must take care not to trip and fall.

Hours would pass, the pressure outside the vehicle increasing as they'd slip deeper into the lightless abyss, scanners probing the darkness in search of something, anything, yet nothing would be found. Not a trace, nor sign of any life, past or present, within the murky fluid, as they draw closer to the core of the mysterious orb.

A sudden ripple, a shudder passing through the surroundings, and seemingly through the very fabric of the ship itself; A touch of true fear now grasping at the hearts of the helpless crew like the cold touch of Death himself. All about, the echo of a single word would pass through the minds of all on board, though the speaker would remain hidden.

Leave. Leave. Leave.

Nothing more, nothing less, though the word strong enough to deaden the minds of the crew to any but that single word.

Leave. Leave. Leave.

Over and over, through the murky void, the unseen speaker's hold upon the minds of those helpless within the exploration vehicle, unable to give voice to the terror within, nor so much as move nor twitch a muscle; The hold absolute.

Leave. Leave. Leave.

Blinding pain would flood their minds, perhaps moments, perhaps an eternity passing before at last unconsciousness enveloped the hapless beings within, the brilliant white light of intense pain giving way at last to the darkness of oblivion.

A single sound echoing in the darkness at last, once time had passed, a hidden, stealthy whisper in the void, a feminine voice, wrought with concern, distant, yet vaguely familiar. A start, and the fellow would shake himself suddenly, as though drawn away from the deepest of sleeps.

A vision? Had that been all? Or had he truely died a thousand depths within that murky abyss, and been reborn once more in the past, with an option to change the future? So many questions, an enigma cloaked in mystery, he drew himself away from the viewscreen, resting a single hand upon the glassy substance for a moment longer before he turned to the droid, a creature of metal and plastic, programmed with the artificial mentality of a Techanoid proper.

A single gesture to brush off the droid's simulated concern, accepting only the printout from its front, and to read silently for a moment. His heart leapt with sudden terror at the words etched upon the page.

"Barren surface... Semi-liquid core... Unknown... Investigate, report back with findings... Departing 0600 hours tomorrow..."
