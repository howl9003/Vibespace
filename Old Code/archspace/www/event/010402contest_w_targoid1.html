The Spawning Pool
by Dylan Hunter


"I,I,I,". Projected by her, and by the others. Darkness, and warm jostling, coupled by a low bubbling sound. And then, release! A disoreinting spin, and finally, splash! into salty water.

"I,I,I". It was blindingly bright, as she automatically projected, only aware that she now existed. But where, or why she existed were questions without meaning to her. The only meaning she could derive was coming from the tumult around her. Massive shapes sweeping above, tiny shapes flitting below... and her in the middle. Along with dozens of other shapes, all ringing out they're cries... "I,I,I".

And then, she was no longer upright. A tail had slapped her, and now she sped towards the blinding surface of the water. Her cell just broke the surface and ran up against an outcropping of rock; it held fast, the mechanisms involved in post-incubation already in motion. She relaxed as she noticed others sharing the same fate as her, and silently moaned as she felt herself churn, and reach out to the rocks. Hours may have passed like this, but so then, could have lifetimes.

Time had no meaning in her fledgling mind. Her clocked ticked nonetheless, and she awoke to find her pod withering away in the blazing heat of the sun.

"I,I,I", her appendages tore effortlessly through the flimsy layer, a new found strength inside of her. But a strength that soon gave way to the first and most important impulse this young one would ever recieve. She was hungry. She did not understand the nature of such a feeling, but her own pre-programmed instincts took over as she made her way over to one of her younger siblings, just newly breaking out of it's pod. She felt the warm blood rushing down her gullet as her strong jaws closed over the throat of her little "brother". Her satisfaction was short-lived, as she felt suddenly, a presence on top of her. Breathing, steaming, clawing biting, projecting "I,I,I". She spun, furious, throwing her assailant backwards, rolling down the stony banks. She leapt after it, a combination of rage, hunger, and instinct burning inside of her.

The attacker now looked quite apologetic, and the female had another meal. The sunlight glanced off of her carapace, reflecting back greens and golds. She felt proud of herself, and her carapace, but again she was interrupted by her hungry brothers and sisters. But this time, it was chaos. Rather than an isolated fight, it was turning into a feeding frenzy, as dozens of hatchlings emerged from their pods looking for their first meal. She fought to keep alive, and to eat. She killed, others died... and the weak were eaten. Such was they way of things."I,I,I". Her preojection was strong, tied with few others.

She was growing, she could feel it. How long had she been in this rock-lined, murky lagoon? She did not know, nor was it a dominant thought. Survival, and hunger took up tha majority of her life as a larvae. Teeth gnashing, claws rending.... siblings screaming. "I,I,I". She could kill many at a time now, and had been recognized by her surviving siblings as a powerful force. Her hunger ebbed however, and was not as frequent these days. She mostly fought to stay alive, and left the kills for others to feed upon. She sat content on her outcropping, projecting. "I,I,I". But she was being approached again. And this time, the competition was not a hatchling, but a senior larvae, not younger than herself.... and just as large.

"I,I,I", it projected as it approached.. she could feel it's hunger, its envy. It lunged at her neck, and it's jaws found their mark. She struggled underneath their power, and managed to flip onto her back. Her fore claws raked the underside of her aggressor. He released her, with deep resentment.

But she did not allow him another chance to attack. She was the aggressor, now. And she leapt upon him with great force, and he yelped underneath "I,I,I", the message resounded in their minds. She tore into this one and drank deeply. Her mind wandering, and expanding with each passing moment.

She was victorious again, and none may challenege her. She rose up, stretching, allowing her newest limbs room to expand. Wings... Glittering, and translucent. She shook them a few times to remove the fluid that encased them. Soon she hovered in the air, just inches above her rocks. And then, she rose. She saw new brothers and sisters below, continuing the cycle. She was in ecstasy, and for the first time, she saw what she was. Saw her other siblings, already matured above her, and around her in the air.

"I,I,-NO!", she corrected herself, " Us, Us, Us,".

And her brothers and sisters acknowledged her, and rang back.... "Us,Us,Us"
