Archspace Alpha Tutorial (Under Construction)
 

Preface
 
Welcome to Archspace. In Archspace, you will develop technologies, work to fortify your military forces, gather resources, start wars on planetary or galaxy scales, and perform various game plays to become the new owner of the universe.
This tutorial is the first guide for alpha test players. We have tried to include as much information about the general overview of the game and play method as possible, but we will not divulge exact formulas or data about the game's algorithms for the following reasons. First, all formulas or numbers are subject to change during the initial balancing stages of the game testing phase. Secondly, we hope that our testers will experiment with different methods to approaching problems and producing solutions, some that our developers may not even have thought of.
Though you may be disappointed because we have told you all the things we WON'T tell you first, the guide WILL contain explanations about the following things. First, a simple walk-through of the game, and second, important rules and key words that will remain intact for the most part through the massive balancing and changes that may come to the game.
Good luck and have fun.
 

I. Walk-through
 
You are a dictator that has just come into power over an entire planet. Or perhaps, according to the species that you have selected to play, you may have been elected by the citizens who have punched their ballot cards in your favor. Your planet is under the influence of an Empire that, at one time, was the authority over all intelligent species in the galaxy. But over countless generations, its power and authority has gradually fallen into ruins. The advanced scientific technologies have all been forgotten and lost, and communications between other star clusters have ceased. You deplore the situation of the galaxy, and wish to restore the Empire to its former glory. Of course, this would all be done under your wise command.
 
Just before jumping into the game
 
Most strategy games have units that a player can command and resources that are used up. The popular game Starcraft, for example, enables players to build buildings and move their military troops anywhere on the map. These buildings and troops act as pawns, and can be considered to be units in the game. Minerals, gases, and supplies are resources that are mined and used to create and maintain these units.
In Archspace, the player must determine the use of and decide the movements for "fleets" and "planets." Planets will be the base for generating resources that a player needs, and fleets will be used to protect or gain planets. In actuality, players have a few more units to control, but they are for the most part either secondary or dependent on the planets and fleets. For example, a player may design and produce individual spaceships, but movements can only occur on a fleet scale. Fleets may be assembled into fleet groups for attacks, but the movement of the fleet group is determined by coordinating and controlling each individual fleet.
The resources that a player gathers and uses are indicated by PP (production points). PP can be considered a measurement of accumulated resources and productivity, but also plays the role of monetary measure within the game. A player's planets generate PP to store resources, fleets are constructed using PP, and their maintenance fees are calculated in PP. Greasing the palm of a corrupt Empire government official also requires PP.
Some other types of resources - For example, this may include the number of citizens residing on your planet, the RP (research points) used in scientific research and development, MP (military points) used for military purposes, and NP (nogada points) used in facility maintenance. These factors automatically increase with time. Some cannot be controlled (population), and some always use the full points available, (RP, MP, NP, etc.) whether it may be too much or too little. The player need not pay attention to these resources, so they are not considered very important.
Time is another resource. Unlike Archmage, the game is commenced with automatic turn updates that occur each set period of time.
 
Login
 
After creating an Archspace portal ID from the Portal Registration site, (you can only play one character from each portal ID), enter your name and select a species from the Create Character menu. Though each species has its own distinctive characteristics, for the purposes of this manual, we will assume that you have chosen the humble and noble Human race. There is no significant difference, if you have chosen another species.
 
Main Menu
 
The portion shown on the right side of the screen is the main page of the game. The main page shows the species logo, important information, and new updates and announcements.
 
Side Frame
 
On the left, the various menus of the game are arranged in a list to facilitate movement between menus. By clicking on the box next to the menu name, you can expand or minimize sub menus under the menu topic.
First, let's start by entering the Domestic menu.
 
Domestic Menu
 
The commands in the Domestic menu are mainly used to control your planets and resource production. The Domestic main menu contains detailed information as well as links to the Domestic sub menus. (These links are identical to the menus listed in the side frame.) The information shown on this page - the number of planets, factories, etc. are the total number of resources and wealth that you possess.
 
Concentration Mode
 
This menu commands the basic development methods for your country. The commands decided here are automatically applied to all your planets. The following lists your options:
Balanced: As its name suggests, this option focuses on balanced and stable progress. This is a good standard option.
Industry: This option focuses on industrial development. The amount of PP accumulated each turn drastically increases.
Military: This option directs your planets to shift to a military mode. You can utilize more resources for military needs than usual. Industrial development and technology research are stopped.
Research: This option focuses on technology research and development.
 
Planet Management
 
This page lists all the planets in your country. (You will probably have only one planet at this time.) There are typically three types of buildings that you can build on a planet.
Factory: Generates PP.
Lab: Generates RP to be used in scientific and technological research.
Military base: Used in planetary defense and fleet maintenance.
By clicking on the planet's name, you can directly control the activities and composition of an individual planet.
 
Planet Page
 
The planet name, population, and PP production rate per turn are always shown, in addition to temperature, atmospheric composition, gravity, and other physical data of the planet. This physical data may differ for each species. The player does not have to pay attention to unfavorable planetary conditions, since the conditions will change favorably through terraforming over time. The special attributes of a planet are factors that influence production rate, so pay special attention to these. The player can change and adjust the building ratio of factories, labs, and military bases. (Don't forget to click the OK button!) Rich or (in very rare cases,) ultra rich planets are rich in minerals, making them ideal for PP production. These planets work best as industrial sites with many factory buildings. Though the number of buildings allowed on a planet may change according to environment or technological skills, there is a realistic limit to these numbers. It would be safer to pursue a steady, balanced development. If you set the investment rate, building production occurs automatically. However, you can speed things up by investing PP. You can invest a large number of PP, but the actual amount of PP used each turn is determined by the economical size of the planet.
 
Research
 
Scientific technology and social development is classified into 4 categories.
Social: Humanities, Philosophy, Psychology, Social Sciences. This branch influences politics and society.
Information: Information Management, Computer Skills. Better computers create a better society (and better weapons.)
Matter-Energy: Physics, Chemistry, Engineering. This branch enables more efficient methods of factory construction and provides the base technology for weapons.
Life: Biology. A wealth of information lies beyond the mysteries of life itself.
The standard research option is Free Research. If left to their own devices, your researchers can progress with research and development at a faster rate than if you designate their research goal and constantly intervene. However, if you have urgent need of a certain technology to be developed, you can choose to focus your research resources on options from a menu containing currently available research options. Like buildings, you can invest in research. PP invested in research is used to increase the RP generated per turn.
We will skip Diplomacy for now and move over to the Fleet option. Please click on the Fleet menu.
 
Fleet
 
In the Fleet menu, you can design spaceships, command ship production, form fleets, and appoint a commander for each fleet. The fleet menu also includes commands and orders to be issued out to the fleets.
The fleet main menu contains a fleet list with each fleet name and current status. You can change the formation of an individual fleet or issue orders from this page. It is possible to change a fleet formation by appointing a new commander, upgrading the spaceships within the fleet (change the ships for ships in another class), or increase the fixed number of ships within the fleet.
You can issue orders to individual fleets. These commands include training, planet stationing, (to prepare for blockades or invasions) planet patrol, (similar to planet stationing but effective for only 24 hours) exploration, (exploring for new planets) and ally reinforcements for alliances that you may form within the game. First, let's expand your territory by exploring for new planets. Select a fleet and choose Expedition. Select Mission, and that fleet will leave on a mission to explore for new planets. If you are lucky, (or if this is your first exploration, definitely,) a new planet will be found. Or they may return with empty hands after 24 hours.
 
Ship Design
 
This command designs "molds," or models to create a ship. You must develop a new design in order to build ships. First, determine the class name and size level. (A gunboat is the smallest size possible.) The size level is the standard size of the ship. The next step is to select the armor, weapons, and equipment for the ship. The bigger the ship, the more variety and number of weapons and equipments can be loaded. However, the cost drastically rises.
When the ship design is completed, a new class is registered.
 
Ship Building
 
This command generates ships using a previously constructed class or other class ships that have been previously designed. To build a ship, simply decide how many ships of a certain design that you want to build. The ships will be automatically generated and placed in the Ship Pool. Like buildings or research, you can increase the production speed by investing additional PP.
 
Ship Pool
 
The military ships that are produced are initially placed in a Ship Pool. The ships in the Ship Pool have not yet been assigned to a fleet. When a new fleet is assembled or damaged ships within an existing fleet are replaced, the ships in the Ship Pool are used. If a Ship Pool is empty and the fleets are damaged during battle, you cannot recover from these damages, so pay special attention and always have sufficient reserve ships in your Ship Pool.
 
Form a New Fleet
 
This command forms a new fleet. Assign a fleet name by designating a prefix and a fleet number. Decide how many spaceships of what classes will make up this fleet, and appoint a commander of the fleet. Be aware that the maximum number of ships allowed in the fleet will be limited by the commander's skill level. A commander's skill level is raised as he/she gains experience.
 
Recall
 
Use this command to relieve a fleet of a certain duty.
