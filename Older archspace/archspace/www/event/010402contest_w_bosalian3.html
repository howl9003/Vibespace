Tatyana's Heritage - The City of Gold
by Dylan Hunter aka "Beram"


Yellow. The colour cascaded from all directions, pouring over the meek form in the center of this whirlwind of energy. A thousand different messages poured into his mind as he floated, helpless and prone. A vision of a thousand golden temples, stretching for miles- And a solitary figure.
Floating above it all, the figure was crying out, the source of the melancholy energy. More cries, pleas for help, flowed from it's glowing form... Incomprehensible, and painfully unbearable. And then, it was clear.

"Help Us..."

Tatyana woke, drenched in sweat. Breathing heavily, she broke away from the disturbing dream. She'd been having many such dreams throughout the past week, ever since entering this quandrant- A quandrant thought to be abandoned. Though she'd become accustomed to the uncomfortable visions, she noted that this particular time was stronger than ever before, and was visibly shaken by it. "Computer", a mechanism blipped in response, "What time is it?".

"The time is 432:7:900, in standard time units, Captain", the computer chirped, autonomously.

Tatyana sighed. 6:30 am, in relative Sol cluster hours. With much effort, she eased her way out of her bed, and over to her lavatory. Looking into the mirror, she could barely recognise herself. But even so, she dutifully washed herself, dressed, and began the short walk to the bridge of the vessel. The Captain noticed, that she was the only one awake. 'Oh well", she thought, 'May as well get started'. She sat down at the Primary control console, and began typing in the necessary co-ordinates to exit the cluster.
However, before she could even place on finger on the command pad, she felt light headed. Her thougts brought her back to her dream, and she could not stop thinking on the message she was given. An overwhelming feeling of intense sadness consumed her, as she sat there alone in the quiet bridge.

Then, she knew where she must go. "Computer", she spoke, "Set the wake-up time for all quarters... right now". She could faintly hear the chiming alarm, as it roused her crew from slumber. Soon enough, she was accompanied on the bridge by two drowsy, and obviously annoyed, men.

"What gives, Captain?", one complained, "You promised we could sleep through the cluster shift!"

"That's correct, Harllin, I did. However, there's been a change in plans- We're not going into cluster shift", as she spoke Captain Tatyana was typing in new co-ordinates.

"What do you mean, 'No cluster shift' ?!", Harllin asked, quite confused.
"We're due meet up with the rest of the Conclave in Isis. We're going to be late already!". The lack of response from Tatyana concluded that she was determined, for whatever reason, and would not be dissuaded. "Where are we going then?", he asked, after the silence sunk in.

"A small planet, just 450 kilounits starboard", She replied, a little confused herself.
The other finally spoke, "Captain, there are NO planets in this cluster, our scans have told us that much". Tatyana finished typing in the co-ordinate string, and it was obvious that there was nothing they could do to change her mind.

"Trust me. There's something I've got to do...", was all she said.

The mercernary ship, Nelwyn, was in visual distance from the mystery planet in less than two hours. The planet was small, and looked to contain atmosphere and water. Tatyana's head was swimming, and the birthmark on her arm ached as the vessel pulled in to orbit the planet.
"What now?", Harllin inquired. "So there is a planet here..." By the look on the Captain's face, Harllin already knew the answer to his next question.
"We're not going to-" Tatyana cut him off, "Yes. We're going down there. It will only be for a little while... Please guys, it's just something I have to do. Harllin, you're coming with me", she glanced at the man beside Harllin," And Samuel... You stay up here, and guard the ship. I'm counting on you".

"Fine with me", Samuel wanted nothing to do with this side trip- or this planet. Captain Tatyana began to walk towards the Pod Room, and her companion grudgingly followed.

Her arm was aching again, as the small Pod craft entered the atmosphere of the planet, and to add to that, she still felt light headed. The craft landed with no difficulty, and its occupants cautiously exited and could not believe the sight beheld to them. From the top of the hill crest they stood on, and for miles into the horizon, were ruins. A sight of great devastation, and of unimaginable beauty.
'Temples', Tatyana thought to herself. But no temples like in her dreams, for these temples were not yellow. Like a moth eaten tapestry this WAS once grand. But now, it was dilapidated and gloomy. Grays were dominant throughout, and a predominant feeling of sadness filled Tatyana's heart, and even seemed to affect Harllin.
"What is this place...?" Harllin's pointless question reverberrated off of the old buildings as the two wandered down the streets.
"This way" Tatyana muttered. So they walked, for what seemed like a lifetime, the level of destruction growing as the moved closer to what seemed to be the center of the city.
The two didn't say much as they moved towards their imaginary goal, but Tatyana's thoughts seemed elsewhere. And then, they saw it. At the very center, there stood a small structure. A pearl in the midst of a dark shell, the structure seemed untouched by the gloom.
"Bosalians..." Tatyana said absently, the pain in her arm eased into a feeling of elation.
"Huh?", Harllin was completely puzzled.
"These buildings.. this planet... Bosalians were once here". Tatyana could almost envision the graceful Bosalian forms, silently skimming the skies between these temples. She gazed at the one perfect structure, and noticed something that sent a stab to her very soul.

On the entrance to the building was a sigil. Tatyana gazed at it, and then at the area on her arm, just above her elbow. They bore the same marking.
Harllin must have also noticed, as he too was visibly shaken. "What it is it...?", he asked.

"It's.... me", Tatyana's statement sent Harllin into total confusion once again. The Captain gave another look at her arm, and then to the entrance of the building. She held her arm up, it began to glow, and slowly the door eased open with an audible groan. She moved towards the opening.

"No, Captain!", Harllin's warnings went unheeded, as his Captain walked inside the structure. He called again, as the door closed behind her.

Hours passed, and Harllin stood with a heavy heart. He didn't know what had become of his Captain, but he had a feeling she was alive. He looked around at the dead city. So much gray. And then, the lone man was overcome with a sense of peace and security. Tatyana, his Captain, was safe- but forever gone from him. His instincts told him that he should go, and so he did. Back throught the streets of the city. His mind wandered on his walk, and his thoughts were dominated by memories of his family, and happy times in his childhood. Though something strange had happened in that building with his Captain, he knew all was well. The city seemed less gloomy when Harllin gave it a final look, when he stood on the crest of the hill, beside the Pod.

And as he began his ascension to the Nelwyn, he could have sworn he saw the city glimmer, bright yellow, behind the shrinking view of the planet.
