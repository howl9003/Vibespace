Renegades
by Carlo Jan P. Garingarao

The Trak'na Star Cluster's neutral zone: stretching more than 15 parsecs of demilitarized space, 3 star systems with a total of 14 resource-rich planets. The vast uninhabited area lay deep within the star cluster, effectively marking the boundaries of the several councils that called Trak'na home.
As far as inter-council peace agreements were concerned, the neutral zone was strictly off-limits to ships, military and civilian alike. Not more than half a century ago, it was an area of intense conflict within Trak'na, each council fighting for complete control of the zone and its wealthy star systems. It was not clear which side was winning, the bitter conflict costing dearly in lives and resources, until everyone realized that fighting for the area was starting to cost them more than they could gain. The Jarnapah Protectorate, a Bosalian council, pushed for a cluster-wide ceasefire, which eventually led to the Trak'na Peace Initiative that effectively stopped the conflicts and encouraged inter-council trade and relations. The zone was soon abandoned, blockaded, all grudges forgiven and apparently forgotten.

Ontonus IV did not even resemble a shadow of the lush, verdant planet it once was two hundred years ago; before the Evintos established its annex in Trak'na. It was a relatively huge planet then, broad continents with fertile land, supporting a highly diverse array of flora and fauna. If left in its own course, with the fostering of evolution, it may have given rise to sentient life in several millenia and perhaps a highly-intelligent race after several eons.
Ontonus IV was a fountainhead cut off long before its prime; converted within two hundred years from a planet of windswept oceans and plenteous terrain into an enormous, gray, metal sphere spinning slowly on its axis, encased by a formidable defense grid keeping a constant vigil over the delicate, sensitive machinery that was the planet itself.
Nine planets in two other star systems suffered the same fate, all forming the local Evintos incumbency registered as  TCS - 289.

Deep in the bowels of the planet, in the command node that had long replaced the once-solid nickel core, a sensor console flashed to life, relaying a lengthy report to the hulking metal form before it.
Multiple field fluctuations in neutral zone barricade, duration: 0.254 trices, point of origin on repulsion grid: X234.4, Y654.2, Z12.7... No vessels detected in perimeter, no stellar bodies in perimeter, no abnormal cosmic events occurring in perimeter...
The Executrix, suspended in the heart of the enormous metal receptacle by countless filament cables linking it to the vast expanse of subsistence systems across the planet, pondered on the latest turn of events. It was, in a sense, the brain of TCS - 289; the sole administrative component that processed all information and issued all the orders in the Evintos annex in Trak'na. The unit was a breathtaking sight in itself: an immense metal sphere one kilometer in diameter, its dark gray surface crisscrossed by relay cables, co-processors and complex circuitry in a dizzying pattern, billions of artificial neurons working non-stop within its colossal bulk.
Analysis of fluctuations: ------------
The Executrix throbbed, lights on its surface blinking in a pattern that may have passed for confusion. This was the second set of fluctuations in the repulsion field within the last two days, an impassable field around the neutral zone the Evintos themselves had established. As before, it was unable to come to a logical conclusion to explain the anomaly.
Relay readings to the other organic life-form administrates in the star cluster?
No. Confirm grid fluctuation readings with detection grid on Ontonus II.
Fluctuations confirmed, sensors on Ontonus II have received identical readings and analysis mainframes yield no acceptable explanations.
This unexplainable phenomenon was unacceptable. The neutral zone was probably being trespassed right under all their sensitive detection equipment, and admitting their inability to account for the fluctuations to the entire star cluster was simply not part of the Executrix's programming.
Launch a DSS probe to site of grid fluctuations immediately, with an escort of three FU combat components.
Units DSS - 003, FU - 2378, FU - 2379, FU  2380 on-line. Launch sequence initiated.
On Ontonus IV's bleak surface, one of the countless component hangars slid open, revealing rows of identical combat components harnessed by slender cables. Mechanical cocoons with their tactual appendages tucked into close proximity, they awaited the Executrix's beck and call. Three FU's abruptly glowed to life, their central hubs blinking a brilliant red, weapon pylons locking into place, propulsion engines humming as they powered up and received the Executrix's orders. The suspension cables snapped loose, and the trio smoothly glided out of the hangar. Converging with the prism-shaped DSS probe seconds later in the planet's orbit, the small squadron leapt into full speed, four metallic streaks on a direct course for the Trak'na neutral zone.

The insignificant metal shard spun lazily in the vacuum of space, buffeted by the solar winds. A slim robotic arm with three outstretched fingers extended towards it, securely clamped it, and brought it under the Evintos DSS probe's close-range scanners. Within seconds, analysis was complete.
Neutronium-mithril alloy, outer hull structural component. One registered spacecraft match with same hull alloy: Dylanus Dominion Ranqul-Class frigate, armed transport vessel. Relaying information to the Executrix...
The probe's escort of three combat components kept a tight ring around the unarmed prism-shaped vessel as it continued its sensor sweep of the neutral zone perimeter. Within thirty trices, two more identical fragments were found and confirmed to be of Xerusian origin.
Without warning, a sleek projectile flew straight for the Evintos ships and shattered in their midst, releasing a brief disruption field that engulfed the squadron. The components froze, their central hubs dimming as the ships' automated functions were scrambled in an instant.

"This is an outrage!" Regent Percinal of the Dylanus Dominion roared, slamming a heavy fist on his throne's armrest. "You expect me to believe that two of my best Teradins, along with twenty Dragoons, vanished without a trace?"
The Xerusian monarch's corpulent form seemed to swell all the more in anger, eyes blazing in rage at the Teradin prostrate before him, helmet nearly touching the throne room's obsidian floor. The dragoon had just brought dire news: the Dominion's unofficial investigation team to the neutral zone send not more than two days ago had utterly vanished without a trace.
"N-not exactly, my lord," Teradin Garpenal groped for words, raising his head as high as he dared, "We have lost all contact with Hieronymus and Valinor for more than two cycles, which is more than past the designated time of return. But this may mean a lot of things... their communication systems may have been somehow damaged, or perhaps they are caught in a middle of a passing meteor storm..."
Percinal raised a hand, and the Xerusian was cut in midsentence.
"Perhapses! Is that all you can give me? Listen to your babbling, Garpenal! Communications on their transport had two back-up systems, and the notion of a meteor storm within a barricaded neutral zone?!?" the regent was leaning forward on his throne now. "Stop giving me excuses, you hortuc!"
Percinal rose, albeit slowly for his bulk, and swept his crimson robes of office aside. "Get up and establish a link to TCS - 289 at once. It's about time I have a word with those spineless serpents!"
Garpenal rose at once, combat armor clanking loudly as he swiftly moved behind a wide communications console to the left corner of the throne room, and pressed a few controls. From the high ceiling, a broad, paper-thin screen lowered two meters before the throne, suspended by a single slender conduit.
"Link established, my lord."
The screen flickered to life, the burst of light resolving into an image of grey, gleaming circuitry with a red mechanical eye glaring in the center: the sensory component of the Evintos Executrix. As expected, the artificial intellect offered no sign of greeting whatsoever, no acknowledgement; the Evintos never saw any use for social niceties.
Percinal narrowed his eyes into slits, and blared at the image, "I suppose I have to greet for the both of us... But let me get straight to the point."
The Executrix awaited silently, lights on its surface blinking in a monotonous sequence. Despite the knowledge that he was conversing with a lifeless chunk of metal and circuitry, the Xerusian could not help but feel uneasy under the red eye's unbreaking stare.
"I need answers, and I will not settle for anything less. Explain the barricade fluctuations in the neutral zone's repulsion field."
 The Executrix instantly replied in a throaty yet clearly mechanical voice, "The Dylanus Dominion has violated the Trak'na Peace Initiative by attempting to send a military transport past the neutral zone barricade. You are responsible for the repulsion field fluctuations. Explain."
Percinal was completely caught off guard. The regent sputtered in outrage, but was at a loss for any words to respond to the accusation. True, he had blatantly violated the initiative by sending Hieronymus and Valinor through the field, but only in the suspicion that the Evintos themselves were transgressing the peace agreement. And he had never expected his little secret mission to be discovered in the first place.
Now, the Executrix had eradicated twenty-two of his finest dragoons, and was turning the tables on the Dominion. The Xerusians were turning out to be the antagonists in this scenario after all, and Percinal had nothing to prove things otherwise.
"You scheming gharjul!" was all the regent could spit in reply. "You planned all of this didn't you? The fluctuations, then luring my men into the neutral zone, then placing the blame on the Dominion! You'll pay dearly for this, I promise you."
The link flickered, and suddenly terminated; the screen turning black and retracting to the ceiling. Percinal turned to Garpenal as he stood behind the console, eyes turning into embers.
"They have cut off all communication links, my liege."
The regent's eyes bored into the Teradin's, "You will mention nothing about what happened to Hieronymus and Valinor to anyone, if you value your life. Put the entire Dominion under full alert immediately, and recall all fleets outside Dominion space. There is no telling what those treacherous tin cans will try next."
The Teradin nodded, bowed at the monarch, then left as quickly as his bulky combat armor allowed to comply with Percinal's orders.

The hangar doors on Ontonus IV's surface reopened, granting access to the three combat components that had just returned from the neutral zone. Suspension cables snaked down and clamped them back into place, slender data-relay conduits attached to their central nodes to download all the information the FU's had recorded during their mission in standard procedure.
 But mission data was not all the combat components had in store within their tampered memory banks, not in the least...

Warning, an emergency console flashed, catching the Executrix's attention, hangars TK- 43, TK- 44, TK- 45, RV - 001 through 015 have been overridden and are no longer responding to command node orders.
Explain.
Unidentified data network virus, originating from hangar RV- 002. It has bypassed standard security scans and is spreading across the interhangar information grid. Long-range communications have been completely scrambled.
RV-002: the hangar housing the three components that have just arrived from the neutral zone. It took the Executrix less than a milli-trice to analyze exactly what was happening. Somehow, a complex information virus had been incorporated into the FU's mission data, and was spreading across the hangars and communication systems like wildfire. It was most likely that the virus was infecting the vessels the hangars housed as well.
Isolate infected hangars, deactivate communication systems, and initiate analysis of network virus.
Hangars isolated, time to complete analysis of virus unknown... Warning, surface sensors detect power build-up within infected hangars: the ships are being brought on-line.
Almost in unison, all the infected hangars exploded outward in brilliant buds of flame, silicon fragments slowly flying from the site in an eerie pattern, due to the planet's lack of gravity. The infected ships contained within - hundreds of combat components and several battlecruisers - came into view, thrusters flaring as the renegade fleets began their slow escape from Ontonus IV.
Should we activate planetary defense grid?
No. The risk of debris damaging our surface systems is unacceptable. Activate grid once the ships have cleared.
If the Evintos were capable of emotion, then this was the epitome of over-confidence. They were so assured of their planetary defense grids that they never bothered shielding the delicate machinery that stretched all across the surface.
The smaller combat components went through the grid first, and were greeted on the other side by the searing phasor beams of the grid's orbital turrets. Instead of leaping into full speed, the FU's returned fire. The turrets were at a distinct disadvantage due to their limited movement, but were heavily shielded enough to withstand a constant barrage.
By the time the Executrix realized what was happening, it was too late. The combat components had diverted the turrets' firepower all too well, leaving the massive battle cruisers to pass through the grid relatively unnoticed. The bulkier ships' engines blazed, and accelerated the vessels out of the grid's reach in maximum speed. The FU's swarmed after them, with only a handful obliterated by the orbital turrets as they fled after the battle cruisers.
The infected ships have penetrated the grid. Calculating all possible trajectories...
It was useless to track the renegades, the Executrix knew. With Ontonus IV's interplanetary communications ravaged by the virus, there was no way of warning the other surrounding planets of TCS- 289 about the runaway vessels before they left Evintos space. And all because of the Xerusians and their damnable Dominion.
Cancel. Prioritize the analysis and destruction of network virus. What is the total number of apostate ships?
Eighty-seven FU-type components and twenty-two DN-type battle cruisers have managed to escape the grid. 
Prepare all assault fleets for launch. Compute least conspicuous trajectory to the Dylanus Dominion... it is time we retaliate.
With a renegade Evintos fleet heading straight for the heart of Trak'na, and TCS-289 declaring war on the Dominion, the peace agreement was all but dead, and the Peace Initiative was about to become a mere memory.
