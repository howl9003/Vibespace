Operation
by Li-Ren Zhou


They were walking around me, models of inefficiency. A human technician was repairing a corvette that had sustained damage a battle with a Targoid. The sluggish movements of his arms and legs only augmented my disgust for the race.

"It was a wonder that they managed to survive the process of natural selection," I thought.

I looked around again and started to walk towards a lone computer terminal near the hangar door.

The computer was not very difficult to compromise, since the humans were still utilizing a version of the electronic computer. Wires extended from my body and rapidly cut into the circuitry of the terminal.

"Wires, entering, piercing¡¦" My mind began to wander for a moment, into the past¡¦

The probe began to slowly push its way into my mind. The dark, cold room created a somber and heartless feeling. The robotic arms and needles caused me to shiver at the grotesque surroundings. I looked to the right and saw the Tecanoid technician staring at me. Nothing reflected from his eyes except emptiness. I turned back my head, just as a sharp needle pierced into my chest. I tried to scream, but nothing responded. The body had become limp.

Though the pain became unbearable, I could do nothing to stop it. I could feel major functions of my body, shutting down. Juices from the needle began to pump into my body as metallic tools cut into my externalities. I could feel the nanoagents sewing the machine onto me, or perhaps me onto the machine. The world became blurred in a wonder of colors from the few lights existent in the room. For a moment, everything became brighter, until I drifted into the darkness again.

The implants opened up the universe for me. Data and information began to fill my mind as I hobbled across to the doorway. I had not been prepared for the new consciousness that lay before me and the implants still acted a bit awkwardly. However, I quickly began to see everything in a new light. I could feel my body moving faster while the world seemed to be moving slower. The universe had become data. Suddenly, I heard a voice ring in my head, "Report to command station one, we need you for a mission."

Arriving at the station, I reported to my superior officer, the commander of the military base on Arebati-273. Unlike before, the room and its inhabitants seemed different to me. Eyes of many other Tecanoids looked at me in envy, for few receive the honor and blessing of the implants. Though it was impossible for a normal Tecanoid to climb up the social ladder high enough to receive the cybernetic enhancements, a highly qualified soldier could eventually gain the gift if he rose in the ranks. I looked back at my commander.

"We have a problem on our hands," he said, "the humans have been slowly moving into our region of space. Even though the recent war with the Targoid had weakened their forces, they still have an overwhelming advantage in the amount of ships."

He looked at me again, and began to send data into my mind.

Everything had been arranged for my safe arrival on the human starbase a few light-years away. I knew what I must do.

I quickly returned from my reflections, just as the end of the data stream entered my information banks. With this information, we could intercept every attack that the humans plan to make, allowing us to ambush the attackers. I turned around again to see the worker, still fumbling over the mithril hull of the corvette. The wires retracted and I started to move away. Soldiers began to swarm around me as the alarm bells rang loudly in the distance. Were it not for the holographic generator that rendered me invisible, I would have been easily found. I walked towards the corvette and attached myself to the bottom. There, I could patiently wait until the ship was far enough away to gain control and return home.
