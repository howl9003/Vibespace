STRATEGY BASICS
 
  Fleet Management:

There are many things to consider when managing your fleets.

- Ship Design:
This is one of the most versatile portions of the game. You have many choices to make. The first choice is which class you will design. For this purpose you may want to consider how quickly you need ships. If you need them faster, then you will want to make a smaller class of ship so you can produce many faster. If you wish to build up a lasting fleet, you may wish to have designs of a few different classes that can cover for each other's weaknesses. Once you have chosen which class to design you must do the actual design. You will have to determine what you need this class ship for. If you need faster ships, you will want to add devices which speed the ship up. If you are more worried about protecting against a certain attack type, you may want to use an armor which works well against such attacks. Finally, you may want to consider who you are most likely to fight. If your enemy is known to use a certain design, you can tailor your weapons and defenses to best take advantage of this knowledge.

- Ship building:
There are three things that can be quite important to be aware of when building your ships. First, you can only combine ships of the same design into the same fleet. Second, you should be aware of how long it takes to build ships. This can be important as mentioned above, but also because later in the game, some of the classes will take the same amount of time to build, so you may want to build a larger class. Finally, you should be aware of how your investment is helping. If you invest PP into your production, you can increase the speed that ships are built.

- Fleet Commander:
The fleet commanders may not seem all that important, but they can be quite a difference at times. You should always be aware of the skills that you commanders have and the abilities that they have. All commanders have some basic skills, but there are other skills and abilities that others do not have. These are usually the background skill and racial skill, but some commanders will sometimes have skills for other reasons.

- Ship Pool:
This may not seem to be an important portion, but there are still some things you should consider. This is where ships are sent if they take damage, but still survive the battle. They will not return to their fleets automatically, so you will have to use the reassignment screen to add them back to their fleets. Another aspect is the fact that you can scrap ships. If you have old ships and do not need them, you may be able to gain emergency PP by destroying them. Finally and maybe most important, you should be aware that ships in your ship pool do not require as much upkeep.

- Form a New Fleet:
This section is relatively straightforward. Two things should be kept in mind. First, you should name then something you can remember. It will save time when assigning them in battle if you can remember what each fleet is specialized for, and which fleets are of the same type. The other thing to keep in mind is your commanders that you assign. Make sure that the skills they have match the purpose of the fleet. It is not good if your main siege force is led by a commander that has a negative siege skill.

- Reassignment:
This is where you can modify fleets in a few ways. You should be aware that many of these changes will cause some confusion as your forces adjust to the changes, so your fleets will lose some experience.

- Expedition:
Expedition is very important when you first begin your game. You can order one fleet to find planets. This fleet will search and then return with a planet that you can then use to expand your power.

- Mission:
There are some things to consider for each of the mission types: Train: This can be important if you have just made a fleet. It can be used to raise their experience without risk, and experience can make a difference in battle. Station on Planet/Patrol: This is an important mission as stationed and patrolling fleets are your defense against piracy. Fleets on these missions also have a bonus in combat as they know the area better. Unfortunately, they are unable to respond to orders, and so they fight under the "normal" battle command. The difference between these two missions is that fleets stationed will remain until you use the recall command, and patrol fleets will return after they finish their patrol. Dispatch to ally: This can be an important mission if your ally is weak for some reason. You can send your fleets to help defend their planets. Like stationed fleets, they will only use the "normal" command, because they refuse to take specific orders from your ally.

- Recall:
This command can be useful after an attack when you are somewhat weaker. You can use it to call fleets back that have been on other mission. This can be useful if you need a general fleet and don't have to worry about piracy. You can then recall all ships that are stationed on planets and have them defend all of your planets. There are three missions that you can recall fleets from: Expedition, Station on Planet, and Dispatch to Ally.
