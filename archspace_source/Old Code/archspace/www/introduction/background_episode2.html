BACKGROUND
 
EPISODE II
 
Aboard the Space Station Archanis
Jorand Torvald, the administrator of the Terran Space Station, drummed his fingers on his belly in a nervous manner. Another resource shipment was about to come in, and with that shipment came the risk of pirate attacks. Out of habit, Jorand's hand reached for the bowl on his desk and snatched up a handful of treats to eat. The sugar certainly didn't help his jitteriness, but he continued to snack. I really need to go on a diet, he thought, as his questing fingers found only air in the bowl.
Suddenly his communications unit snapped to life, as the head of security, Jamesson, reported in.
"Sir! The convoy is appearing at the outer edge of the security zone. I am vectoring the security forces to them to provide protection on the run in." the security chief said.
Jorand, not being trained in military tactics, deferred to the chief's decision, and returned to watching out of the window. He was the first to see the pirates descend on them.
The security chief's decision, while great for the convoy, laid the rest of the area defenseless. The pirates somehow knew to exploit this, and their ships roared in from the opposite direction. The first ships in were a pair of fast light carriers, closing to short range before they launched their complements of fighters to harass the station and keep the gunners occupied. The flanks were help by several pairs of pirate corvettes that brutally attacked everything that tried to break past them. In the first few minutes, six light freighters were destroyed or crippled as they attempted to run the blockade. Down the center came the pirate battleship, with a triad of cruisers as escorts. They set about methodically shooting the engines out of the larger ore haulers and large goods transports. The pirate ships, though antiquated by modern standards, were more then a match for the unarmed ships they found. However, a pair of freighters turned towards the blockade and suddenly jetted forward much faster then any normal freighter could ever go. As concealed weapons opened up, explosions lit the freighters. As the fake parts came off, the lines of a pair of the Terran Empire's newest destroyer type came to view. The two destroyers were painted with a special infrared and sensor-deadening black paint, instantly identifying them as being assigned to the Terran Commandos. The two destroyers, moving nearly as fast as the older pirate Corvettes, blasted towards the nearest flank, a flotilla of civilian ships following in their wake.
Using their superior firepower, the Commando ships blasted through the pirate line easily, and the civilian ships followed through the resulting hole. Many of the ships disappeared at once, running up to lightspeed on the first course they could get. The Commando ships, however, swung around, flanking the attacking force.
Meanwhile, the convoy that had just arrived moved slowly towards the station, the station's defense force arrayed around it. Again and again, the captains of the smaller Corvettes and Frigates asked Security Chief Jamesson to release them to go help the beleaguered station, which had all three cruisers battering it with their massive MD main cannons. Meanwhile, the pirate's battleship and the two carriers started docking with the crippled ships, transferring loot over from the ships to themselves.

Aboard the Destroyer Intrepid
"...Very good, Warlord Chiax. I thank you for your contribution to my retirement fund." Jamesson said with a smug grin. So much money towards his retirement! At this rate, he would be able to retire in another year- two at most. And then he could live like a king on his poor, downtrodden homeworld.
"And thank you for the successful collection of our due. Let's do this again. I'll contact you by the normal means." The pirate leader, Chiax, said, before cutting the link. Just in time, as Jorand overrode the communications line.
"Jamesson! Why aren't you dealing with the pirates!" Jorand said frantically. The image flickered slightly as a pair of pirate fighters strafed the station, nearly hitting the communication equipment.
"They will be dealt with¡¦soon." Jamesson said, then cut the link. Soon, of course, the pirates would leave, and Jamesson would be able to cite his need to defend the convoy. Suddenly, the communications channel was again overridden. The sender was one of the Commando Corvettes.
"Jamesson! What the sanskhyd are you doing! Attack the pirates at ONCE!" the communications officer on board the Corvette screamed.
"Yes sir." Jamesson replied with a barely civil edge to his voice. The communications officer on board the Corvette was only a sergeant, but like all members of the Commandos, he was allowed to give orders to anyone below Admirals rank and expect to have them obeyed. Jamesson reluctantly released the Frigates and six of the eight corvettes to go and engage the pirate fleet. These ships went straight up the center, counting on the constant fighter harassment of the station's defense complement and the Commando ships on the flanks to distract the pirates enough to allow the frontal assault to succeed. Since most of the pirates' main guns were occupied with tying down the space station, the tactic worked long enough for the Frigates and Corvettes to hit the pirates hard.
Suddenly, the sensors blared out a warning- enemy ships right behind! For behind the convoy, a Targoid war fleet had appeared, with the distinctive cruiser Ka'chak in the lead. Behind it there was four more cruisers, with various lighter ships right behind. The fleet was headed by Chi'raka, one of the most effective military mind-extensions ever produced by the Targoid race.

Aboard the cruiser, Ka'chak
Chi'raka sent the appropriate instructions to the rest of the fleet, and the fighters and light warships swept ahead, towards the convoy. They had orders to flank the enemy, allowing the enemy to expend shots trying to hit their swift and maneuverable ships, while the main cruisers closed to attack range. Chi'raka's cruiser bore down on the single human destroyer, while the other four Targoid cruisers turned towards the paired corvettes and the freighters. It was no battle, really. The warships fled before the might of the Targoid fleet, leaving the freighters to die. However, one of them, breached in several areas from the hits it had taken, turned and rammed the nearest Targoid cruiser. The ships blew up one after another; the freighter's cargo of munitions blowing up several nearby light ships.
The pirates slowly started to withdraw, wanting nothing to do with a well-armed Targoid invasion fleet, which would blow even the pirates mighty battleship to pieces within a few minutes. However, the Terran fleet continued to attack the pirates from all sides, and the withdrawal bogged down as the pirates had to fight the destroyers, frigates, corvettes and fighters assaulting them. Being flanked at every turn, and the frigates and corvettes having just passed through the center of their formation delivering a double-broadside, the pirates decided they should finish this battle before running, simply to prevent one of their precious capital ships from being crippled and destroyed.
Then the Targoid fighters approached and tipped the balance of power. The Terrans were trapped between the Targoid light ships and the pirate fleet, which was loading its fighters in anticipation of retreating. Suddenly, the fighters were ordered to launch again, as coming from the direction the pirates had first attacked from came three massive ships- a pair of Targoid battleships and a fleet carrier. Suddenly, the pirates and the Terrans were trapped in a pincer movement between the Targoid fleet's lighter ships and its heavier battleships and carrier. One of the Commando destroyers was the first victim of the Targoid's newest formation- the two battleships battered it into twisted metal within minutes. The Targoid fighters, outnumbering the Terran and pirate fighters combined, sweep the area around the space station clear of all enemy fighters, and started to strafe the lighter ships of the enemy formations. The Intrepid met its end when the Ka'chak blew a hole through the human destroyer with its heavy forward MD cannons. The pirate battleship tangled with the two Targoid battleships and was left burning in their wake. A few pirate and Terran ships, their enmity forgotten in the terror of the Targoid attack, banded together and managed to flee the battle, but for the rest, there was only death.
Jorand Torvald faced away from the viewport of his office, unwilling to face his end. Seconds later, Targoid weapons fire destroyed that level of the station, before breaching through to the reactor core at the center of the station. The station exploded in a fiery flower of light, and in its wake, the system fell into the claws of the Targoid, and into peace. The peace of death.
