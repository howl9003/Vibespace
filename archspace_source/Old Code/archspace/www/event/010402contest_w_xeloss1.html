Cold Rage
By Steven Lourie

...it is interesting to note, that the words "massacre" and "hatred" did not exist in the Bosalian language until 378 UC, the year we first encountered the Xeloss...
                                                                                              - Excerpt from a philosophy
                                                                                                 lecture on Bosa Prime

 Horus Setepenre stormed from the Empire council meeting, his stiff hide boots striking the metal floor in a thunderous cadence. Every two steps a hollow sounding noise was heard, the staff of his office as Lord Bishop adding its wooden voice to the dimly lit hall. Horus's dark crimson robes flowed and swirled behind him, such was his haste to leave the council chambers, away from the place of veiled threats and half-truths. The Lord Bishop's grey face was shaped into a bitter sneer and any race with psionic power would see a churning storm cloud of energy hovering over his aura. The hall lead from the special speaker pod in the gigantic Imperial Audience Chamber straight to the airlock where the Lord Bishop's personal ship, the Redemption, sat idly awaiting his return.

  A pace behind the fuming bishop were two of Horus's elite guards, who, while keeping pace with their liege, the sound of their footsteps were drowned out by all the noise Horus himself was creating. They remained impassively silent, used to their liege's temper, and each of them bearing mental and physical scars from when the Lord Bishop's anger had been directed upon them. More motivation to remain "useful" to the elder Xeloss, lest his next rage tear them apart at the seams.

  A low-ranking Xeloss crew man stood by the airlock, the faint light and the distance couldn't hide his trembling from Horus's eyes. He had reason to be afraid; the roster list aboard the Redemption was constantly changing as Thralls, as the lowest caste of Xeloss were called, were constantly meeting grim fates should they be present when the Lord Bishop received bad news or they had failed in a duty, however trivial. Horus toyed with the idea of slaying the Thrall, or better yet, having the Thrall slay himself as a sign of devotion, but he just wanted to be on his way. The warnings he had received from the Magistrate about leaving mutilated corpses on the station, framed by an eldritch web of their own bodily fluids, were bothersome to read and return with some semblance of an apology attached. The airlock doors hissed open with the sound of hydraulics and the small party walked through.

  Once aboard the Redemption Horus remembered something else about the meeting that had angered him and he started to swear aloud as the three stepped into an elevator and ascended silently to the bridge. " Fools. Cowards. Heathens. Liars! " the bishop ranted, shaking his fist at the door as if it were the thing causing him such rage. The elevator trembled in its path as a sudden flare of psychic anger from Horus caused the electromagnets to lose power for a moment. The two guards looked at one another, wondering if their decades of devotion and extreme martial training were to end in an elevator crash. The Lord Bishop calmed himself somewhat, taking the time to more objectively review what had caused this most recent burst of anger.

  Acharya. Acharya Sahitya. Horus would see that name wiped from existence before his life was through. It was that infuriating Bosalian Peace-Crafter (such was the term for a Bosalian diplomat) that had enraged him the most. As he had for years past, Acharya was constantly touting the benefits of strategic disarmament and a proposed " Star Shield Accord " which would make hostile actions from any faction in the empire lead to the eventual total dissolution of their military power, in addition to severe cut backs in military spending. Such was the Bosalian penchant for weakness. The stubborn Acharya, in spite of being in a tiny minority that supported the accord, had even recently begun to increase his lobbying, making

  Horus wonder if there was some sort of time limit set on the Peace-Crafter to push the accord through. Horus had had this thought in the Imperial Audience Chamber as well, which had prompted him to send a psychic probe at the Peace-Crafter when a flicker of doubt and depression had entered into his aura, weakening his defences for just a moment. There was panic at first, there always was. No race had ever felt comfortable when suddenly you are sharing your mind with an unwelcome intruder, not even the Bosalians, who freely used psychic communication and stood by all their "one with the universe " drivel. Horus had faced the aged Bosalian in the swirling, nebula-like psychic landscape of the Peace-Crafter's mind, reaching out with tendrils of energy, trying to wretch information about why the other was so desperate to get the accord agreed upon. Horus also sent crushing waves of fear towards the Peace-Crafter, hoping to weaken him further. A feeling of triumph quickly soured into surprise as the Bosalian, shrinking back from the assault, suddenly straightened up, his aura completely restored. The tendrils were still attached around Acharya's mind, like psychic ropes that held the two together mentally. " So, you wish to know my mind do you Horus Setpenre? Very well, let me show you what I am thinking of right now. "

  With this, the psychic landscape rushed away from the two, while another image rushed in to fill the void, like air into a vacuum. Horus found himself standing in a huge park of sorts, filled with brightly coloured flying reptiles, serpent-like insects with gossamer wings shining brilliantly in the two suns high over heard, and natural plant life abound. Groups, couples and even a few solitary Bosalians moved about the park, the groups conversing in gentle voices about freedom, peace, and life in general. In a small amphitheatre constructed of shining white stone a teacher was speaking to a large audience about slipping free from negative feelings and desires, and about how to truly love another being without doubt or suspicion ever troubling you. It was almost more than

  Horus could stand. He immediately recoiled, trying to draw his feelers from Acharya's mind and leave, but he found he was held fast, the accursed Bosalian using the strength of Horus'own probes to hold him there. Horus screamed aloud and violently cast his mind out, finding himself dazed and sitting in his speaker pod. The whole exchange had gone unnoticed by most of the others there, yet there were a few who had sensed the amount of power being used. Horus looked down upon Acharya to see an aura of contented calm around the Peace-Crafter, who met Horus's eyes and sent a psychic whisper to the Xeloss Lord Bishop; ' Peace be with you Horus Setepenre, though a stranger it may be. '

  The lift came to a gentle stop, the two guards relaxing visibly in relief that they had reached their destination whole of mind and body. The doors opened, but Horus hesitated at the portal. The smugness with which Acharya had spoke to him still burned at his soul, dark thoughts of vengeance and torture mixing within the Lord Bishop. Given time for preparation and to enjoy himself, even the Bosalian's mighty powers would eventually yield to him, and then he would take great joy in tearing every good and decent memory from Acharya's mind, leaving nothing but a blank and dark void. Horus actually smiled at the thought as he broke himself from the reverie and moved off the elevator. He took his seat in the center of the bridge, reclining in a chair that was both smooth and comfortable, yet possessed large wickedly curved spikes around the back arcing towards the front. Resting his staff in a nook of the chair designed for that purpose, Horus looked almost calmly out of the view screen at the front of the bridge his anger somewhat appeased by thoughts of torturing the Peace-Crafter.

  The Redemption shuddered lightly as it disengaged from the docking clamp, and the view shifted as the ship gently moved away from the station and started to face the way back home. The Lord Bishop sat up rigid in his chair as he noticed something on the monitor that caught his interest. " Hold our position! " he ordered sharply, and the Redemption came to a slow stop.

  Dead ahead was Acharya's ship, also turning itself around and plotting a course for home. It was dead in his sights, at this close range the other ship would have no room for evasive manoeuvres. Even as psionically shielded as the ship was, a concentrated assault from the much larger Redemption would reduce Acharya's ship to space particles in a matter of minutes. All Horus had to do was give the order. He relished the thought of destroying the other ship like other races would enjoy a quality beverage, rolling it around on the pallet before finally taking that last swallow. There would be fuss of course, much fuss, but Horus was sure that more than a few council members would applaud the concept of not having to hear Acharya preach on at every meeting, and an excuse for the ship's destruction could be found easily enough. The Bosalian ships started to move away from the station, getting ready to use the full power of its engines to speed away. The bridge was silent, all awaiting Horus's command, whatever that might be. A slow smile started to spread across the Lord Bishop's grey face, a low chuckle escaping his lips. " Take us away from this nest of fools, Thrall! Lest weakness be catching like a disease. "

  With that the Redemption finished turning, and after reaching a safe distance, created a warp bubble around itself and shot off a blinding speed back to Xeloss space. Horus leaned back in his chair and crossed his long fingers before him, looking over them at the stars that were little more than streaks of light across the view screen. If vengeance was a dish to be served, Horus would make his a seven course meal. Instant destruction was for the feeble-minded and base, while waiting for the right moment to squash an annoying, stinging insect, even if that means being hurt in the mean time, was well worth the wait.
