Mother Knows Best
by Steven Lourie


The planet of Sokar-223 is completely uninhabitable, period. Whatever mineral resources hide underneath that fetid swamp that covers 96.5% of the planet's surface is in no way even equal to the projected costs it would take to mine it and haul it off planet. Of the three survey teams that were sent out into that green hell only one came back, and even then three of them later died of some new strain of Raging Fever that as immune to our best gene therapies and medical technologies...

Thoughts of building structures on or around the many small mountains that jut out from the surface in hopes that they would remain stable should be abandoned immediately. These "mountains" are little more than glorified mud hills, and most of them have a habit of suddenly exploding as gases underneath eventually build up and obliterate the hill and anything that happened to be on or around it. We think this is how one of the survey parties was destroyed...

In my earnest opinion, starting a mining operation on Sokar-223 would be the greatest act of lunacy since the expedition sent out to determine Agerus genders (may peace be with those poor souls). If, despite my vehement objections, such an operation were to start up, I assure you I will be handing in my resignation, and so will most of my team, contracts be damned.

- Excerpts from a letter by Norman Dupin,
Chief Surveyor, Atlas Mining Company.


A Mist Fish ducked in and out of a bank of thick grey fog, lazily flapping its large transparent fins and twisting its body so that it could propel itself through the air by flicking its tail from side-to-side. It was a species unique to Sokar-223, capable of flying through the air, lifted by the gases that constantly bubbled up from decaying vegetation under the murky water; or swimming where the waters were open enough to permit such methods of transportation. The Mist Fish was currently doing what gave it its name, half-flying and half-swimming through a patch of air super-saturated with condensing water. Neither an air-breathing nor a purely aquatic creature could survive in those patches, yet the Mist Fish had adapted perfectly, making it a marvel of evolution and catching the attention of any of the few explorers that had managed to pierce the forbidding interior.

The Mist Fishes' eyes were focused on the waters beneath it, looking for tell-tale ripples that revealed the presence of edible insects or other aquatic denizens of the swamplands. Just then, vibrations both in the air and in the water caused the creature to dart back into the interior of the mist bank, as the noisy intruder passed by.

"Ara Thak! Where is that mountain? Tullj! Get our bearings! "

The commotion that had interrupted the Mist Fish's hunt for food was a full squad of Xerusian Dragoons storming through the waist deep water and mud, on the run. The group of eight stopped, six of the Dragoons creating a defensive ring around the last two, their leader and the communications officer. Tullj MajQa , a veteran of six campaigns and with sixteen years combat experience under his sword belt, felt like a new recruit as he frantically touched tiny keys on his wrist locator to try and get a link with the Xerusian ship that floated in high orbit over the planet, hoping the signal could penetrate the foliage and give them a location relative to the ship. After a few tense moments Mel'zek K'mpec, High Commander of the squad, barked again at his comm. officer to get their bearings.

"Due east...I think. Sir."

Mel'zek turned to face his comm. officer square on. With his powered Eternium armor glistening from the humidity, the huge Xerusian was the picture of sternness. It was said that he wouldn't allow his troops to die if they hadn't filled out the proper forms, such was his dedication to the Soldier's Code, and he certainly wasn't the sort who accepted an 'I think' as enough information to operate on. Tullj shrank back somewhat from the imposing Dragoon commander, quickly checking his wrist link again.

"Due east sir. No doubt about it."

Mel'zek nodded once and looked up from the cringing comm. officer.

"We've got ten minutes and three miles of waist-deep sludge to wade through, Dragoons. If I were with any other squad or any other race, I'd be worried. We're going to make it to that landing point and we're going to make it look easy. Dragoons forever!"

"Dragoons forever!" his troops echoed. The squad was back in its running formation within three paces and the Xerusians left the area, leaving nothing behind but churning water.

When at last the Mist Fish had decided that it was safe enough to make an appearance again, it drifted out, searching the area for any sounds or smells that might herald an attack. When all was calm again it floated out and resumed its hunt for food. An instant later it was in its death throes as a set of Targoid mandibles crushed its light skeleton easily. The deep green Targoid, hovering above the water on two pairs of rapidly flapping gossamer wings, tossed its head back and swallowed the Mist Fish whole. Seven others like it, all of the Warrior genus, appeared out from the upper canopy, sensitive biochemical sensors located in the upper torso detecting their quarry. They had passed through here not long ago. By some unheard signal, all eight suddenly flew upwards, rejoining the rest of the swarm.

Swarm Mother Krhrhuukhahath Bmataurhammazikhhathahat sat motionless far above the ground below, awaiting the return of her children. It was determined by the Prime Swarm that it would be her brood that would hunt down the alien interlopers, whose ship had crashed into the watery surface of the planet five miles to the west. It had long since sunk into the sludge, its great weight driving it down until nothing could be seen of it.

The Swarm Mother was a fearsome sight to behold. Her body was five times as large as that of her Warrior children, and much different in structure. While the Warriors were vaguely humanoid, she was a flying barge of super-hard chitin, monomolecular-edged pincers and bristling psionic power. She was at the pinnacle of Targoid gene therapy, her basic body structure having undergone hundreds of hours of controlled mutations and genetic recombination. Her children, all except the small Probes which were little more than floating sacks with dangling antennae and vestigial limbs, had all undergone the blessed therapies, making them nigh-perfect as a species. There was one more honor to be bestowed upon them, however, and this mission would see the fulfillment of that dream; Mother Krhr would be given a Seed Ship and her brood allowed to colonize an entire planet, creating a whole new breed of Targoid. This was her wish, to have her children go forth and overrun the lesser races until nothing but the Targoid existed.

With the return of her Warrior children, telling her with pheromones and empathic impulses that they had sensed their prey not far ahead. Mother Krhr considered the direction in which the fleeing organisms were headed, and quickly arrived at the conclusion that they were making for a large mountain to the east. All species wished to leave this place, not capable of adapting to the planet's specialized environment, and just like all others, they would not be allowed to.

The Mother sent a swarm of Probes up ahead to track the prey more closely and moved her bulk forward with the aid of psionic repulsion and lighter-than-air gasses stored in sacs on her sides. The prey was organized, moving in unison and not giving into panic like so many had before them. The Mother would have to study them more to determine a weakness, and through a combination of pheromones and telepathic communication she told her advance scouts to reveal themselves to the prey and for several of her Warriors to harass the group and discern their reactive tactics.

Five Probes banked down out of the upper canopy and flew straight for the prey. The Probes were harmless, unable to attack, but they were useful pawns for tactics such as this. Still out of sight range of the prey, the Mother smelled the death chemicals released as her Probes died, six discharges of energy heard in the swamp stillness. The death of her lesser brood concerned her not, they were easy to replace. There were more shots fired as her Warriors moved in, and it disturbed her this time to smell more of the death chemical released by wounded Targoid drift up to the sense cluster located on a slender stalk above her shapeless head.

They were dangerous, these ones. Of the three Warriors, only one escaped, but that was all she needed. It spoke to her in its simple yet efficient language, saying that when threatened the prey-swarm formed a ring around two members and kept moving, protecting them. In spite of their unnatural technologies, other races reacted to life-threatening danger in much the same way; protect that which gives life. The protected ones must be the head, their queen-mother. Without her, the swarm would falter, and then fall.

Mother Krhr sent out more communications, telling her Warriors to mark the two protected organisms with special scents that would guide the other Warriors to attack only them. Another signal told a group of five Warrior children to flank the prey to the right, the Mother using her expert knowledge of the area to her swarm's best advantage. Now was the time to strike.

"Ara Ara Thak! What did those bugs spray me with? " Mel'zek roared, trying in vain to wipe away a greenish sludge that had coated his power armor. They had hit his comm. officer too, Tullj trying to focus on his wrist link rather than the threatening hum of dozens of large wings in the foliage above the Dragoon squad. The Soldier's Code strictly forbade unnecessary weapons fire, so the squad had to wait for a sighting before they could shoot. These bugs seemed to know their Code, however impossible that seemed, and flew so close to them that he swore he could feel the air flow from the wings, and yet blended in so well it was impossible to get a clear sighting.

Suddenly the foliage from both sides of the squad erupted as two of the grey flying lumps flew at the party. Two shots of plasma burst them apart easily, yet a fraction of a second behind them were two of the flying spiked ones. The Dragoons readjusted their sights and fired just as a squad member from the rear was hoisted aloft by two more of the green-armored flyers and carried out of sight. The two green flyers in the front died as well, but one of their squad was gone. Jumbled, frantic calls over the comm. link suddenly went dead and the Dragoons nervously looked at the gently swaying greenery around them. The buzzing returned, louder than before. Another shape burst out of the plants to the left of the squad, and four plasma launchers fired in perfect unision. The charred remains of the lost squad member sailed past as six more of the green flyers dropped down from directly above and snatched Mel'zek and Tullj and carried them up. The prey screamed and fired at the flyers, killing the two whose only purpose was to guard the other four so that they could escape.

With the queen being torn apart by her Warriors a distance away from the rest of the swarm, Mother Krhr told the remainder of her Warriors to chase the prey into the sinking bogs to the left of their position, where the swamp would finish her task for her. Far above the planet's surface the mighty Xerusian battleship was being torn apart by endless swarms of much smaller WarDrone ships. Nothing of the crashed ship or the rescue attempt would be found, just as had happened so many times before.

Mother Krhr floated above the corpses of her dead brood, nothing any other race would register as regret passing through her mind. She would have her new planet, and her children would spread out across the cosmos. They would all heed their Mother's orders unquestioningly, even if it meant certain death. After all, Mother knows best.
