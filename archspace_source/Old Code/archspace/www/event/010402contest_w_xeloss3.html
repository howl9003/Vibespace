The Trak'na Chronicles: SALVATION
By Carlo Jan P. Garingarao

"This is a war-torn galaxy, Prelatess," the deacon observed, grimly watching the countless shards of debris drift past the wide viewport. "Perhaps even more so than our own. Just look at the devastation that fills this system." Bathsheba nodded, keeping her crimson robes close to her slender form, as if to keep the scene that surrounded them from touching her. "Such poor life-forms indeed; unguided, godless. It is fortunate that we have arrived in time, to keep these bloodthirsty heretics from destroying themselves."
A large sheet of scorched hull spun dangerously close, then bounced off as the shields easily deflected it. To the far right of the view-port, an enormous battlecruiser floated by; useless, more than a dozen gaping holes punched through its thick outer hull. At the sight of the ship, Bathsheba's eyes narrowed and glowed with restrained fury. "A Xerusian ship... those heathen swine are here as well?"
The deacon's clenched his hands into tight fists, "It appears so, your worship. They have probably tainted this system with their godlessness. We should purge the entire cluster of their influence!" Despite her rage, the prelatess raised a calming hand to him, "Not too fast, Tul Merok. Perhaps we should give the others a chance to be saved; offer them salvation from their doomed ways."
Tul Merok bowed in apology, then asked, "What should we do, Prelatess?"
"Onward, my child," she crooned. "Towards the vessel they call Central."

The Initiative Convention Hall had never seen such a gathering since the signing of the peace agreement fifty-two years ago. It was a vast, round chamber with a thick, transparent, domed ceiling; the seats arranged along the room's perimeter; the entire hall situated in Central's topmost level.
But the gathering was far from orderly, as delegates from the Initiative's member councils raised their voices, shook fists, gestured in disagreement, or merely covered their heads in frustration. The emergency meeting had started quite civilized half an hour ago, but eventually ended in accusations, pointing fingers, and vehement insults... all focusing on the neutral zone skirmishes in the past cycle.
Jehrom, Bosalian ambassador of the Jarnapah Protectorate and current Overseer of the Trak'na Peace Initiative, mustered his psionic voice and projected it into the congregation's minds, "Please, let us all focus on finding a solution to this misunderstanding..." "Misunderstanding?!?" Teradin Garpenal planted both hands on the ring-shaped table. "I have never heard a graver understatement to a crisis! The Evintos have blatantly violated the peace agreement, destroyed two of our elite squadrons two cycles ago, declared war on the Dominion, and have been assaulting our peripheral colonies! And you call all that a simple misunderstanding?!?"
A Human female to the enraged Xerusian's left rose, "He's right. This must be treated more than a petty argument. This is full-fledged war we are talking about, and right within the Trak'na Peace Initiative. The Evintos must be made to explain their sudden offensive against the Dylanus Dominion."
"Which is exactly why this emergency meeting has been called," the Bosalian thought in reply. "Now if you'll all please calm down and sit, we shall discuss this matter clearly."
The assembly quieted down, and Jehrom continued, "All right. First of all, the Evintos of TCS- 289 have declared war against the Dylanus Dominion for no apparent reason, the Xerusians claim."
Garpenal shifted uncomfortably in his seat, eyes flitting from side to side as the entire convention turned to him. "That is correct... two of our best Dragoon squadrons were mercilessly ambushed by the Evintos in a routine border patrol." "And exactly where were they ambushed?" a Buckaneer hissed.
"Outside the Neutral Zone. It is clear that they had no reason to attack unless they were hiding something of immense value within the neutral zone."
"Are you suggesting that they have violated the peace agreement?" a Tecanoid to the Human female's left asked tonelessly.
The Xerusian teradin nodded, "There is no doubt. And because we have uncovered that secret, they have attempted to invade Dominion space. By the regent's sword, can't you all see? It is as clear as daylight! We have to stop TCS - 289 before they begin setting their sights on the other councils! "Two of our peripheral colonies have been abandoned due to their advances, one overcome by the Evintos, many of our fleets destroyed in border clashes," Garpenal continued. "How much more proof does the Initiative need?" "All right, let us vote on the matter," the Bosalian agreed. "Should the Initiative take action against TCS - 289 or not?" The results were not a surprise: all voted for action against the Evintos incumbency. Overseer Jehrom concluded with a downcast tone, "Then it is settled. As much as we despise an offensive stand, we have no choice but to launch an all-out attack."
For the next two hours, plans for a special Initiative armada was complete, consisting of numerous fleets from the different member councils of the star cluster. The roster of fleets was registered, assault plans concluded. By the time the matter was closed, the chamber's atmosphere had calmed down to an agreeable level. But the station's klaxons blared without warning, and a voice warned, "Warning, unidentified vessel approaching Central, dropping out of transdimensional space. All weapon and defense systems on-line." At once, the relative calm the assembly had enjoyed erupted into confusion. Jehrom leaned towards the comm on the podium before him, "Do not fire unless provoked. Is the vessel armed?" "Extremely armed, Overseer. But weapons do not seem to be powered up... yet." At that moment, a deep shadow crept across the convention hall, as a large object loomed directly overhead the chamber, blocking the light of a nearby star. Past the transparent barrier that formed the domed ceiling floated a ship of immense proportions. It resembled a cluster of towering spires and turrets linked by conduits, all erected atop a massive base of glowing metal.
"My God," the Human female gasped. "It's as large as Central..."

"Impressive," Tul Merok smiled faintly as Central came into full view. "They have activated their weapons, Prelatess." From their overhead view, the Initiative station was a sight to behold: A massive, spherical central hub of light grey metal, surrounded by an outer ring linked by spoke-like corridors. The outer ring itself was studded with four, thick, defensive pylons lined with weapon slots. "No matter, their defenses won't stand a chance," Bathsheba smiled as well. "Come, my child, let us offer them a chance to join us in salvation."

Never in her wildest dreams had Cosette Knox imagined a meeting in Central to turn out like this. It was ironic, recalling how she had been chosen as one of the Dupre Autonomy's delegates to the session as a sort of 'reward' for eliminating the Agerus threat at Firma a cycle ago, a respite from her duties as commander of the DMC security forces in the mining system.
She peered up at the behemoth vessel that overshadowed the chamber. Beside her, the Tecanoid Kel-Ten, her conn officer in Firma, intoned, "Commander, perhaps we should return to our ship and leave." Before she could reply, a brilliant light flashed in the middle of the chamber, resolving into two tall, slender figures garbed in shimmering robes with golden sashes. One was in red, the other in purple with an elaborate headdress atop his/her head.
At once, almost everyone in the room had withdrawn their weapons, and pointed them at the newcomers. The one in red raised a long, wiry hand, and the hands of those who had armed themselves sprang painfully open, dropping their weapons on the floor instantly.
"They're psionic. Bosalian rebels?" Cosette asked Kel-Ten in a low tone, glad that she had not withdrawn her tachyon pistol along with the others.
The Tecanoid replied, "I am not certain. Their physical structures are vastly different."
The one in purple spoke in a soothing voice, "Greetings, I am Prelatess Bathsheba of the Order of Yuragon. Do not be alarmed, we offer you salvation."
"Salvation from what?" a Buckaneer delegate hissed, massaging his taloned hand.
"From your wicked, godless ways, of course," Bathsheba replied matter-of-factly, as if the answer was obvious. "The gods have sent us to cleanse this galaxy of unbelievers, as we have in our own. Submit to our guidance, and you will be spared from their divine wrath."
Overseer Jehrom interjected, "Our cluster does not need your guidance, or your gods. We have managed to co-exist without any outside help, pardon your intrusion, and kindly ask you to leave."
Bathsheba and her companion glanced at each other, as if communicating without words, then the other one laughed and spoke in a raspy voice, "Do not fool yourselves and try our patience. We have seen the signs, the conflict that tears your cluster apart."
"And so you come bringing more conflict, fanatical partisans!"
It was the Xerusian Garpenal who spoke in a booming voice, stepping forward towards the pair with his blade while tapping a control imbedded into his gauntlet. The two seemed to swell in anger at the sight of the teradin, and were visibly surprised.
"So," the one in red snarled, "you are all in cohorts with this heretic? No wonder you have become as damned as these Xerusians!"
"You know them, Garpenal?" Cosette frowned.
"All too well," he replied without taking his eyes off the two. "They are the Xeloss, an overzealous race following an insane, bloody religion of death and sacrifice. They are the reason why our galaxy was consumed by the void, and earned our undying enmity."
"Woe to you all," the Prelatess shook her head as if in mourning. "We came too late. Now we shall have to destroy you all, you give us no choice."
Jehrom quickly spoke into his comm, "All weapons, open fire. Disable the unidentified vessel." No reply came.
"Do not waste your time trying," the one in red sneered at the Bosalian. "Your men have been properly taken care of." In cue, Central's defensive shields flickered, then were shut down from within. The station was helpless, its men held in the Xeloss' psionic grip. Without warning, Garpenal powered up his anti-matter blade and charged at the Xeloss pair with a battle cry. Tul Merok raised a hand at him, but nothing happened. He barely had time to evade the blow, and screamed as anti-matter opened a large wound at the Xeloss' upper arm.
"You should know better than that," the Xerusian growled. "Your tricks don't work on me." He charged again, but fell back as something large crashed into his chest and brought him flat in his back on the floor... the Buckaneer ambassador. "What are you...?!?" Garpenal cried out as the Buckaneer wrenched his sword out of his grip, pinned down by his weight and his own heavy combat armor. His eyes were unnaturally bright as he mindlessly tore at the Xerusian's armor with his talons.
Tul Merok grinned, "Yes, but they work on the rest. Watch as your own allies slay you." He raised his hand again, now pointed at Cosette's direction.
"Stop!" Overseer Jehrom came to Garpenal's side, and all of a sudden the Buckaneer stopped its assault and staggered to the side of the chamber holding its head. "This has gone far enough!"
"Ah..." Bathsheba's eyes lit in realization, facing the Bosalian. "We share the same gift. It is a pity it cannot help you save your precious comrades."
The Prelatess and the Overseer faced one another, engaged in a silent, invisible struggle. The Bosalian's psionic aura flared a brilliant blue, engulfing him with a radiance, while Bathsheba thrust both hands at him. With a shriek, she staggered back, exhausted by the mental battle. Tul Merok now entered the fight, wiry fingers curled into fists. Jehrom merely stood glaring at the Xeloss, and his neck twisted with a sickening crunch, and he fell to the floor with his crimson robes swirling around his corpse.
Bathsheba's eyes glowed with fury, then she was wrapped in a white light and vanished from the convention hall. A deafening silence filled the chamber at her departure. Jehrom shook his head in revulsion of his actions, "She is right, there is nothing that will stop them from destroying Central. Our weapons are useless with our men forced into refusing to fire them."
Overhead, the enormous Xeloss ship's weapons glowed into life, aimed at the Initiative station. Then came a miracle. A horde of Evintos combat components and battlecruisers came into view of the domed ceiling, and began pelting the Xeloss vessel with phasers and anti-matter missiles. It was clear that the Xeloss was not winning, their psionic devices unable to control the mechanical Evintos.
"Overseer," a voice crackled through the comm, "we have regained control of the station. I'm re-activating defenses." The battle raged on over their heads, illuminating the convention hall as Evintos ships detonated at the Xeloss weapons-fire. In a minute, half of the Evintos fleet had been destroyed, but the Xeloss had all but lost.
"Open fire at all targets," the Bosalian ordered wearily. "Destroy them all."
A missile salvo from the Initiative station devoured the Xeloss ship in a firestorm, and it exploded with enough force to rock Central. The weakened Evintos fleet was decimated fifteen minutes later, with the station sustaining only minor damage. Unwittingly, the renegade Evintos fleet had helped save the Trak'na Peace Initiative from the unexpected Xeloss threat. Cosette returned her attention to the weary congregation around her, then at the unmoved Kel-ten at her side. Surprisingly, the Tecanoid had remained at his seat throughout the entire battle.
Jehrom's voice concluded, "All things set aside, the Initiative Armada will assemble around Central exactly three days from now. The Evintos may have unintentionally saved us by engaging the Xeloss, but that does not excuse them from the fact that they have broken the peace agreement. This session is closed."
The Bosalian locked his eyes with Garpenal as he rose from the floor and retrieved his blade. The Xerusian held the gaze for a second, then lowered his eyes in shame.
He was fortunate that Jehrom was unable to read his mind, but felt dishonorable at having fooled the entire Initiative that the Evintos had violated the treaty first. But he had no choice... to disobey Regent Percinal's orders meant treason.
