The Asteroid Belt
By Jonathan Kimak

We had finally done it. Our first extra planetary probe launched with success and was now 1 million miles from where I stood in the observatory. "Now we can finally get a good look at that asteroid belt" mentioned my colleague Norlo Freit. The asteroid belt, yes, thoughts of finally seeing it up close have kept me sleepless for the past few months and finally it is a reality.

We discovered the belt 5 years ago when we had just finished creating our planet's largest telescope ever, able to view half a million miles into space, it was then that we first noticed the belt. The asteroids were amazing, they did not encircle anything, they were lined up perfectly in rows upon rows of more rocks. They were not affected by the gravity of any of the surrounding planets, and just remained in one place, unmoving. That was when I was a junior member of the Creakiol Space Exploration Team(CSET). When I first gazed upon those glorious asteroids I knew that I had to see them up close, and through my initiative this probe that is now out there was built. The senior scientists said there were more important things for me to be concerned with such as the increasing volcanic activity on Creakiol II, the closest planet to our home planet Creakiol. But Shal Nudor, the Karlok, our planet's leader was caught up in my enthusiasm when I had the honor of meeting him. And so I was quickly promoted and began work on the design of the probe, and now, 5 years later I am the chief scientist of CSET and my dream has taken flight.

"Norlo, I'm going home for the night, keep me posted the instant the probe picks up anything." "You bet boss" he mentioned, he knew I didn't like being called boss, I was happy being known as Redrick. I went home to my house but did not wake my family. I could not sleep, the visions of what I thought the belt would look like up close would not leave my mind and consumed most of my thoughts. So I spent the night in my chair, staring into the sky, with my head pointed towards the belt.

The next morning when I returned to the observatory Norlo met me with an unusual expression. "Boss, you got here just in time, we just got some preliminary data from the probe and a look at the video feed, you have got to see this." I followed him into the conference room where a projector was set up. On it was the video feed from our probe, it was amazing. The asteroids were much larger than I had thought and seemed to be getting larger. "They're growing?" I asked. "No boss, it appears that way because they are actually moving, towards us, towards Creakiol." "How can that be, they have been stationary for at least 5 years if not much more." I exclaimed. "I don't know but they started moving 2 hours ago when the probe was 100 000 miles from it" Norlo said.

My joy had suddenly turned to shock and terror. "At there present velocity how long until they reach the planet?" I asked. "3 days, 4 tops boss." I knew we had to act quickly, forgetting for the moment the question of how and why the asteroids began moving. "I'll alert Shal Nudor, you start preparing scenarios of what can happen if those asteroids don't stop moving and end up hitting us." I was frantic and now I had to tell the leader of our entire planet that we could be hit by a cataclysmic event worse than the past millenniums of war combined.

I informed Shal Nudor of what I myself had just learned minutes ago. He was not scared at all. He had an aura of pure peace and calmness which finally made me realize why he was our leader. "What do you suggest?" he asked. I didn't have a clue as to what we should do so I told him to come with me back to the observatory, he agreed.

When we arrived Norlo greeted us with more of a grimace than a smile. "Boss, honorable Shal Nudor, I've already created 3 scenarios of what we could do if the asteroids hit." He spoke with a slight tremble in his voice. "First I thought of using the underground villages that all 3 continents created during the Brigali War when there was a high threat of nuclear attacks. The problem is that at the current velocity of the asteroids, they would penetrate the planet straight to the core." "So what is your second scenario?" Shal Nudor asked.

"Well this is what I believe to be the best course of action, we still use the underground villages but we also fire a full Nuclear arsenal at the asteroids when they are closer to the planet. If we calculate it correctly, the resulting radiation would be dissipated in space that the planet would not have a severe Nuclear winter and the population could return to the surface in 5 years. The problem with this plan though, is that we would need people around the continent to stay on the surface to aim and fire the missiles, since automation would be much more risky." "And your last plan?" I asked. "Pray" is all Norlo said.

"Very well, we shall go with your second plan Mr. Freit" dictated Shal Nudor. And so the preparations began.

The underground villages were scattered around our planet's three continents and could hold more than our current world population in them. They would need to be restocked now, but once that happened they could support life for 10 years so our plan should work. But those who stayed on the surface could not then enter the villages, as they are sealed shut for the entire 10 years so as to prevent anyone for wandering out and exposing themselves to possible radiation poisoning. The villages also had a communication network with cameras around the planet so that we could still see what happened on the surface and communicate between villages.

It was now one day until the asteroids would be upon us, and only 7 hours until we would fire the missiles. I had volunteered as one of the people to stay on the surface, as did Norlo. We had more than enough people willing to sacrifice themselves for our planet which made me feel more patriotic than ever. Everyone who was not a volunteer was now in one of the shelters, including my wife and daughter. I was allowed to use to communication network to send them a final message but could not bring myself to say goodbye, so I did not. Hopefully they will understand.

"Norlo, are all the targets acquired yet?" I asked impatiently. "Almost boss, over 10 000 asteroids accounted for and 2 000 more to go, it's going to be close." As the time slowly moved on I began to feel a little bit of peace, not quite on the level I had seen in Shal Nudor, but peace none the less.

The time was upon us now. "Begin firing sequence" came a voice on the comm system. It was Admiral Drekno, the man in charge of all surface people. I went to my station and prepared my set of missiles, 100 in all. It wouldn't be long now until we would find out if this would work. There was an uneasy silence after everyone announced readiness. "Fire" came the command and the sky lit up with thousands of missiles heading into space. I quickly ran to the room that housed the telescope and looked up towards the asteroids. The missiles were almost there when something awful happened. The asteroids stopped, and then moved aside so that all the missiles passed them by. "This can't be happening boss, how did that just happen?" Norlo was frantic.

The asteroids began moving again, but now they lined up into rows like that of a military platoon. We then received a voice transmission over the network, it was in our language but it sounded as if the speaker had just recently learned to speak.

"Thiss is Duralos of the Agerus, this planet is ours. We brought your ancestors here many thousands of years ago so that you would one day join us when you advanced enough. I am leader of the observation team that has watched over you since the colonization."

I did not believe what I listened to, I had heard many years ago about the myth of Agerus but everyone knew it to be false. At least we thought it to be false.

The voice continued, "When we saw your probe we knew you were now advanced enough to join us and learn the ways of our people and fight our enemies for us. But such an act of betrayal as to attempt to murder your own saviors cannot go unpunished. Therefore you are sentenced to death except for a few that we will select to relocate to a new planet where this time you may learn obedience.

And then the asteroids were upon us, blocking out the sky why their size. It was then that I noticed that these asteroids had a flesh like appearance around most of their surface. As they swarmed around our observatory and smashed it down with me in it I remembered what the meaning of the word Agerus was in our language. It meant "Death."
