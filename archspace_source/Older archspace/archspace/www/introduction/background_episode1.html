BACKGROUND
 
EPISODE I
 
Lythos breathed in the thick air of Expenatu Polis as he gazed upon the brilliant purple sky. Though his lungs, pampered by the ideal conditions of the Empire's library, were sorely protesting the 3G atmosphere, his senses were more than happy to be alive and feeling again. Polis wasn't home, but it was where he had lived for the last 14 years. That is, until he received the Empire's calling to take a librarian's position...
But no such feelings were to be found on Lythos' face as he walked along the magnificent hall of the palace. His sparkling green-grey eyes were a stark contrast to his face, stiffened into an expressionless mask, while his blond locks swung lightly with each step. These were the only traces of evidence to show that he was a living, breathing person. While the familiar metallic footsteps of the security robots behind him rang low, Lythos thought back to his childhood memories of the director's warm smile. He was affectionate and kind, always smiling, and provided everything that Lythos - and all the other children - needed. All except for love, that is.

"You are finally back." Though his raven hair was now sprinkled with gray, the director had not changed a bit. Always his warm smile, with the ever present tone of authority in his voice. Appearing to genuinely welcome home a son that he had not seen in a very long time. However, still no sign of affection or true emotion could be felt. Lythos knew it very well. "It's been a long time, father."
Lythos had also not changed a bit. As always, his eyes radiated respect. As always, he was ever modest and deferent in his manner. The only thing that had ever changed was the guard robots behind him. They formed a group of two each, covering each other's blind spots to form a perfect 360 degree view. These robots were assigned to both present and retired librarians of the Empire, and represented the ultimate power of the librarians. Behind them were the Empire, and the Empire's library. And Lythos was representing that power in a meeting with the one whom he had called father all his life.

"So you are leaving, as planned."
After completing the simple but intense meeting, Lythos had arrived at the launching pad to go on vacation to his home planet. He immediately recognized the deep voice, and turned around to face his "brothers and sisters" who had come to bid him good-bye. The same age as Lythos, Sarmick's dark brown eyes glowed with an intensity that only his siblings could recognize. Sarmick's burning ambition matched the color of his fiery hair. It was this ambition that had carried him thus far, to the position of strategic planning officer. If not for Lythos, he would surely have been named as librarian of the Empire. On the other hand, if not for him, Lythos might have been assisting the director. Forced into separation from their parents under the cause of raising superior talent, this group of elite workers were raised with the director's elicits funds. Now they were trying to cleanse their corrupt childhood with their own hands. The director was an influential man, and it was not easy to contact all of the siblings, who were spread out across the galaxy. But even the omnipotent director would not be able to realize that not one or two but most of his "children" were plotting to rebel against him. Everyone had carried out their tasks well. The only thing left was to sit back and wait.
"I have relatives back home," said Lythos dreamily.
And his relatives had massive armies. Originally, his family was one of the top families of the planet Syphrian, but with the information and appropriate orders provided by Lythos, his family had all but taken control of the entire planet. The military forces of Syphrian were small in size, but they possessed the most up-to-date weapons based on the technology that Lythos had acquired from the Empire. In addition, the soldiers were excited about the prospect of the new experiences to come. The other planets that Lythos' siblings controlled had similar appearances, with slight differences in weaponry. Though he was already the leader among his siblings, he had the additional goal of assembling outside forces for this mission. This was made possible because he had no direct position within Expenatu Polis. His siblings would take care of the work within the Polis itself.
"Farewell. Congratulations on your great accomplishment."
"We shall celebrate when I return. We need a party in order to make a toast."
The conversation was not continued. It had been too long since communication was lost between the siblings, at least in appearance. They could not exchange many words. And there was no need to do so, either. They were not reveling in the reunion of past memories, but rather reconfirming the will which held them together. A new world was waiting to open in front of their eyes.

Three months later, Expenatu Polis was surrounded by hundreds of battleships. Most of the fleets were made up of Curvet type gun ships, which were relatively weaker than most other fleets. But this could not be helped. If cruiser ships or other large ships had been placed in that position, they would have experienced gravitational problems due to their excessive mass and weight. It was important to show number, not size, to the naked eye. The director would surely know that all of the surrounding ships were from his territories, and most of them from the Expenatu Polis security patrol fleet. It was true that the battleships that Lythos and his siblings had assembled could not compare to any one of the main forces of the Expenatu Polis military. But the main military forces were far away on "new" missions that the strategic planning department had devised for the director. Victory was an almost certain possibility, and would be a great help to the expansion and development of Expenatu. The strategic planning team accurately predicted to the director that this risky standoff with the all-Tecanoid forces would be well worth the gamble, since there were no outside forces that could attack the weak points of the planet. All the information and analyses that had been provided to the director were accurate. Except for the fact that a rebellion was brewing right beneath him.
A small scale skirmish was in progress, across the lavender sky. The Curvet fleets were being bombarded by battleships which were small and swift, with surprisingly heavy firepower for their small size. On the other hand, massive cruiser ships in dense formations struggled with the low pressure gravity. Both sides could not use their military strategies with these unexpected conditions, but it was more extreme in the case of the rebels, which had been assembled from different planets and thus lacked unity in formation and movement. They had come all this way without knowing their clear purpose in battle. The Expenatu defensive fleets were not having it as easy either, as they were overwhelmed by the sheer size and firepower of the opposition.
While the defensive fleets were frantically holding off against attacking forces, ground forces that Lythos' siblings had personally raised were swiftly and promptly taking over the palace. The goals of the rebel fleets were to attract the attention of the citizens and buy precious time for the ground forces to act. The head of the Royal Guard was a childhood friend of the director, and a bloodthirsty battle maniac. He had anticipated this much of a loss, since he failed in the control of the royal guard's forces. Instead, he had planned for a large-scale fleet battle. While the royal guard pilots, possessed with a sense of heroism, were overwhelming the rebel fleets with their sheer numbers (and wasting precious time), the ground fleets had immediately landed on the surface of Expenatu Polis upon their arrival. They had seized central controls and were busy pursuing the director in order to arrest him. Their dark blue fatigues closely resembled that of the royal guards, but were imprinted with a special pattern. It would be difficult for the royal guards' goggles to detect this pattern without the attachment of a special filter.
The ground forces were also a unified group gathered from different planets, but they had been intensively trained in their specific tasks. They were an elite militia that had been mentally trained personally by Lythos' siblings. Six months had passed since secret training grounds -- exact replications of Expenatu Polis' palace -- had been established on the five planets that had come under Lythos' influence. Not only the lead commander, but each and every soldier knew every nook and cranny of the palace. These soldiers were all filled with the determination to drive out the corrupt director and establish a new government for the people. They were ready to commit their lives to this cause.

Lieutenant Commander Gismere of the 2nd Battalion, Battle Unit of the Royal Guards, passed through the thin atmosphere of the stratosphere, while dodging the blue ionized crossfire with practiced skill. He approached the sleek, silver destroyer. Arriving with little difficulty, he fired his rail gun with expert accuracy, but the artillery capacity of his gun's cartridge fell to 0 before he could penetrate the electronic barrier. He had no choice but to retreat back to the planet, as most of his units had all but used up their firepower. But there was no guarantee that he and his units could retreat and come back safely, as they had lost all communication with the planet's Central Command Tower. In addition, who would be able to fill his place of command? Once he left, a gap of many minutes would leave a void in the planet's defenses. He was the one and only commanding officer left in space.
The Royal Guards' small but strong attacks were rapidly decreasing. They had suffered heavy losses in battle, but more importantly, they were running out of ammunition in their fighter planes. A fighter plane had room for only one pilot, and sacrifices all function save offensive attack capabilities. The firepower of a fighter plane can match the strength of a Curvet ship ten times its size. However, it's small size limits its artillery capacity. Unless weapons are constantly supplied by a mother ship or other military bases, its attack powers cannot help but rapidly decrease. The rebels had already used their sheer numbers and force to grasp command of the space surrounding the planet, and use that advantage to take over the planet's capital. The problem of weapon supply was desperate for the Royal Guards, who were relying on ground support.
The battle within the palace was dragging on, with no clear advantage on either side. The director did not know that there was going to be an internal rebellion, but he knew what to do in case one happened, and the Royal Guards were trained periodically to handle such a matter. The director personally armed himself, and with a few Royal Guard members, used the complex structure of the palace to freely move and try to escape from his attackers. The rebel army's commanders could not help but be flabbergasted, as they had received orders to capture him alive. To add to their difficulty, the head of the Royal Guard, as well as some of its officers, were forming similar groups and performing the similar actions all over the palace. Though the rebels had been trained well in the structure of the palace, and were led by Sarmick, who knew the palace structure well, they were hopelessly led into a game of hide-and-seek.

"This is taking too much time"
Lythos said quietly as he faced the monitor in front of him. He was in the director's strategy planning room. Standing behind him, his siblings exchanged nervous glances. The aerial attack was a mere diversion, and had succeeded beyond expectations. The key places of the palace had already been seized. All that was left was the capture of the director. One hour had passed since orders had been carried out, but still no news. Though he had been caught off-guard, the director proved his abilities. He was the man who had started out with one planet and expanded it to over thirty planets spanning the entire cluster. This was not something achieved by sheer luck or fortune. If the director could overcome this situation and escape to one of his other planets, what would happen? But this was not the only reason why Lythos' siblings were nervous. Lythos, who would soon be the new director, was expressing discontent and disappointment in them.

The director destroyed the switchgear of the security door. As he narrowly escaped the eyes of rebel troops, he moved swiftly and re-checked the remaining firepower of his MD pistol. Though much heavier and less focused in impact than a beam weapon, a pistol had more fire capacity, and could fire more rapidly and accurately than a beam weapon. This made the pistol a perfect defensive weapon. The blaster range showed that there would be approximately four or five shots left on the weapon. One bullet could make the difference between life and death.
Four shots left, and three people remaining. It was the perfect number of shots to end it cleanly and with dignity for himself and his men. But if the director had been as weak as to contemplate suicide, he would not been able to achieve such a vast conquest of planets. Sweat was pouring down the ebony face of the director, but he and his men kept on moving. Soon, the scout he had sent ahead signaled enemy sighting, and the director and his men swiftly hid themselves. The rebels wore special uniforms linked to their vital signs. Should they die or faint, their status would be swiftly reported to their leader. It was impossible to switch clothes and disguise themselves as the enemy. The only solution was to eliminate the enemy one by one, while constantly moving through the maze-like structure of the palace, until they could escape. How many more would have to be killed before escape was possible. The director was not the type to ponder how long he could hold out until he was captured.

Upon landing, Lieutenant Commander Gismere, who had returned to the palace to restock the ammunition on his ship, was immediately ordered to disarm himself. Though he had anticipated this sort of action, he could not help but feel indignant. A fighter pilot had his pride, and the only time that he would admit defeat was when a bullet had pierced his pilot seat. No humanoid in the entire galaxy would welcome the thought of surrender at gunpoint, especially since the pilot had returned with his plane intact. But Gismere, along with the other pilots, received the same treatment upon their return from a futile battle. A war cannot be won on will alone.

The intricately decorated wall burst upon flames as it was pelted with heavy blaster fire. Thick smoke started to cover the air with its toxic fumes, and the director and his men began to retreat. A rebel leader detected the movement of the three targets through his sensor, and ordered his men to fire. Having lost their sense of direction due to the smoke and the flashes of light as the rebel men began their rapid fire, the targets began to fall one by one. The first order of the "new director" was to shoot and kill the old director. Lythos had concluded that it would be dangerous to drag the chase any further. He had planned to kill the director, anyway. Of course, he wanted to personally see the defeat on the face of the director, but that was a personal vanity. He could not afford to indulge his personal feelings and put the fate of greater things at risk. As the smoke cleared and people started to restore order, the rebel leader confirmed the faces of the enemies, and was overwhelmed with surprise and joy. He opened a communications channel, while still not believing in his good luck.
"Target captured."

Expenatu Polis reclaimed its order. The fire was put out, bodies disposed of, and a new law and order was restored to the planet. The people of the planet did not really care who took control of the government, and soon subjected to the winner. Those who opposed the new leader were swiftly taken care of. Among them was the fate of an ambitious man, who was betrayed by those he trusted, and was denied the fruits of victory. The other siblings did not possess enough courage or skill to challenge him. Three days later, the newly appointed director sent a letter to the battleships who were by now celebrating their victory and starting to head home. He could not be sure whether they would agree to its contents or not, but now his job was to make those who disagreed follow his will.
"Rebellion occurrence in Expenatu Polis. Leader Sarmick and other rebels captured and executed. Regretfully director dies in disorder. Following director's dying wishes, successor has been named through meeting of the people. Director, Lythos Asametian Syphrian"
