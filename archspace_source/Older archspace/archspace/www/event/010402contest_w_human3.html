The Trak'na Chronicles: "ET TU, GARPENAL?"
by Carlo Jan P. Garingarao

"Hello there," she greeted the Human female standing before her with a half-hearted smile. "Aren't you a sight for sore eyes?"
The woman before her was quite young, about her age, actually. It was the deeply-etched lines of exhaustion and lack of sleep that seemed to double her age; dark, distinct bags swelling beneath her eyes. Despite all she'd been through, she still managed to keep herself together, she was a DMC commander, a good one at that.
Cosette Knox straightened her uniform, grinned as wide as she could to stretch her face, gave the mirror one last critical gaze, and exited the lavatory. She was still in command; Agerus usurpers, Evintos aggressors, and Xeloss fanatics be damned.
"Commander, we have entered Dupre I orbit," Kel-ten's voice echoed across her cabin in cue. "Initiating docking procedures... the admiral also wants a word with you once we dock."

Dupre I, capital world of the Dupre Autonomy, a two-ringed planet in the Teron star system. The Autonomy itself was a relative newcomer council to Trak'na, established less than a century ago by Tecanoid and Human insurgents who had dared to question the Empire's dictatorial rule and lost. Now the Autonomy was quite well settled in the star cluster, its territory expanding over three systems, its relations with the other councils peaceful (the recent Agerus conflict, perhaps, as an exception).
The DMC Command Station loomed before Cosette's frigate as it spun slowly over the planet. Despite its official name, the Station was actually an enormous mobile fortress, the biggest of its kind in the DMC armada. It had been constructed in the years of the Neutral Zone conflict, even though the Autonomy never got actively involved in the war. Thus, it never actually left the capital world's orbit, and its designation as a station remained.
Cosette watched as Kel-ten adeptly steered their vessel past the usual orbital traffic weaving between the Station and Dupre I. Her Tecanoid second-in-command, what with his numerous cybernetic enhancements and bionic upgrades, was very well capable of running the entire frigate on his own if the need arose.
She stole a glance at the security console to Kel-Ten's left; empty. Vernon's death during the Agerus conflict in Firma seemed like a dream now, a faded memory. Things just didn't seem quite the same without the nonchalant Vernie; she even fancied that Kel-ten, apathetic as he was, didn't seem as blunt as he used to be. The trio was never going to be whole again.
On the viewport before her, two docking guards waved strobe rods flashing ruby red, guiding their frigate towards its designated berth deep beneath the command station's bulk. The vessel slipped past the other occupied quays, shifted into position, and was secured by three magnetic clamps that snaked from the station's hull.

"I see..." Admiral Pascoe nodded thoughtfully once Cosette concluded her report on the Initiative meeting, eyes focused somewhere on top of the table between him and Cosette, bushy eyebrows furrowed in contemplation. The admiral, a lean man of about sixty years, had gray hair streaked with silver at the sides, a long, beak-like nose, and a moustache that somehow managed to stay black. "And the roster of fleets for the assault force?"
Cosette flipped her infopad open, displayed the information on its thin screen, and handed it over to the admiral. "I've uploaded a copy into the station's data banks, sir."
Once more, Pascoe nodded and grunted in affirmation as he scanned the list. "When will the Initiative Assault Armada be assembled?"
"Three days from now, at Central. Overseer Jehrom himself will lead the entire armada into TCS - 289."
The admiral raised an eyebrow, "A Bosalian leading the armada? He's better off as a diplomat, not a general."
"He seemed capable enough, Admiral. Considering how he managed to defeat the Xeloss at Central."
Pascoe handed her back the infopad, stood, and turned to the wide port on the bulkhead behind him, which gave one a stunning view of Dupre I and its intersecting rings. "It was fortunate enough for him - for all of you at Central - that the Evintos made their attack at that time. It is one thing to defeat an overwhelmed foe, another to besiege a formidable adversary in his own dominion.
"I want you to lead the Autonomy's ships in the siege of TCS - 289, commander. Who knows what Jehrom might do... not that I do not trust the Bosalians and their Protectorate. Their exaggerated sense of pacifism might cause unnecessary losses. Go along with his plan, as long as it is reasonable. If ever you feel he is being too conciliatory in his tactics, do as you see fit."
Cosette opened her mouth to object on the admiral's distrust, but thought twice and shut it. Pascoe was just doing his job; taking good care of his men. If she was in his shoes, she would've probably given the same orders, if it meant saving the lives of her fellow marines.

The time had come at last. A total of thirty-seven fleets from almost all of the Trak'na Peace Initiative's member councils, more than fifty-thousand individual ships of various class and size. Dylanus Dominion garhams, Jarnapah Proctorate mediators, Dupre Autonomy lancers, Amalgamate corsairs; all assembled into a vast armada that had departed from Central three hours ago.
Overseer Jehrom's formation was centralized in nature, the different fleets arranged in a circular array. In the middle were the Bosalian mediators, along with the Protectorate's dreadnaught flagship, the Retribution. In a ring around the Bosalian fleet were the Dominion's heavily-armored garhams, and in another ring father out from it the Autonomy's well-armed lancers, and on the outermost periphery of the armada, the smaller corvettes and frigates of the other councils.

"We are entering TCS - 289 territory now, Overseer," one of the Retribution's navigators detached himself from the enormous ship's psionic drive grid, and reported to Jehrom. "Sensors detect a large Evintos armada heading straight for us, mostly battlecruiser-class vessels."
The Bosalian commander replied, "Link me to the entire Initiative armada... Attention, we are about to engage with Evintos battlecruisers, hold your positions. Open fire in your own discretion, but DO NOT break formation."

The battlecruisers seemed to appear out of nowhere as they dropped out of hyperdrive, and began pelting the advancing armada with a hail of phasor beams and anti-matter missiles. Streaking past them in dizzying speeds, the smaller Evintos combat components joined the fray. In response, the smaller ships of the armada's outer ring engaged with the combat components, while the Autonomy's lancers showered the Evintos with tachyon beams. Despite the greater damage the battlecruisers exposed themselves, they still advanced towards the lancers.

"The Evintos are still progressing towards the Autonomy fleet," Kel-ten turned slightly towards Cosette from his console. Cosette gripped the command chair's armrests tight, uncertain of her next move. The lancers were mowing down the Evintos wave, but they just seemed to keep on coming despite the increasing damage they sustained. Her capital ship happened to be situated on the other end of the circle, far from the point of conflict, but that did not mean she could allow her ships to be placed at risk. She had to do something...
Just before she made up her mind to break the ring formation, a line of the advancing Evintos armada vanished in a brilliant conflagration: they had finally entered the Xerusian gahrams' weapons range. The gahrams were launching their neutron missiles from behind, along with the lancers' tachyon bursts.
Teradin Garpenal's harsh voice cut into the command channel, "Jehrom, the other half of the armada is useless in this formation of yours! We should break off the rear and allow them to join the battle!"
Cosette frowned at the Xerusian commander's tone, but he had a point. The other half of the ring formation was out of range of the Evintos armada. But the Overseer's firm voice replied, "No. Keep all your ships in formation, the Evintos may have a second wave in store. We cannot let our guard down."
"Preposterous!" Garpenal insisted. "Those metalbrains aren't capable of such..."
Right then, an Evintos fleet of dreadnaughts materialized at the rear of the Initiative, weapons blazing. Cosette's ship bucked slightly, but its shields held. She quickly ordered the rear half of the lancers to open fire, as the other ships of the armada engaged the hulking dreadnaughts. Now, the Bosalian mediators unleashed their weapons, hurling extra-dimensional matter at the massive ships with their oscillators. Despite the surprise attack from the rear, the armada was quick to respond.
In half an hour, the battle was over. The Initiative armada suffered miraculously slight losses, while the Evintos defense fleets were, it seemed, obliterated. Cosette could not help but praise Jehrom's tactics. The ring formation worked; if any of them had broken off, it would've resulted in disaster. She felt foolish in considering to overrule the Overseer's command, when it had saved them all.
But the Bosalian apparently wasted no time in lauding, "If there are no other pressing matters, let us advance. Plot a direct course for Ontonus IV, central nexus of TCS - 289."

Ontonus IV, heart of the Evintos incumbency, lay suspended in the dark veil of space, a gray metal sphere completely converted by the Evintos into a colossal machine. The entire Initiative armada now encircled it, all weapons pointed downward, sensors keeping a watchful eye on the surrounding space.
Something was wrong, Cosette thought as she eyed the planet on the ship's viewscreen. There was virtually no resistance at their arrival, not even a single weapon fired in their proximity.
Kel-ten concluded his sensor sweep of the planet, "Commander, almost 87% of the planet's internal systems are off-line. Detailed scan reveals five thousand operational ships on the surface, but they are also off-line. The orbital defense grid has been deactivated... or damaged."
"Keep scanning for any abnormal activity," she nodded.
Once more, Garpenal's voice boomed over the link, "What are we all waiting for? This is the perfect time to end this conflict, let us destroy the Executrix while it is at its weakest."
"No," Jehrom replied. "Something is amiss, it almost seems as if the planet has recently suffered from an assault. We cannot establish communications with the Executrix and should send a team down to investigate." "Investigate?!?" came the Xerusian's mocking remark. "We came here to eliminate the Evintos threat to Trak'na peace, and here you are suggesting that we negotiate with them?"
Cosette entered the conversation, "With all due respect, Garpenal, I agree with the Overseer. I'm going down as well." The other council commanders concurred and in the end, the entire armada agreed to send a small armed team into the Executrix's chamber itself: Garpenal, Cosette, Kel-ten and two Conglomerate Buckaneers.

The team phase-shifted into a wide metal catwalk following the chamber's perimeter. They were deep within the planet's bowels, where the core once was in fact, before the Evintos planted the Executrix in an enormous metal shell in its place. "We are high above the Executrix," Kel-ten needlessly informed all of them. "It is down there, 500 meters below." Garpenal grunted, approached the railing and peered at the long drop below and everyone followed suit. To their surprise, the gigantic metallic sphere that was the Executrix lay on the curved bottom of the chamber. Normally, it would've been suspended in the middle of the room, held in place by more than a million data-cables. But now, more than half of the cables seemed to have given way and snapped, resulting in its current position. It's red processing node was barely visible from their height, facing downward.
"We have to get down," Cosette looked around for an elevator, but found none.
The Buckaneers offered their help, and aided her and the Tecanoids in descending as they glided down the chamber with their leathery wings. Garpenal merely snorted at them, tapped his leg armor and leapt from the railing without hesitation, his landing softened by built-in gravity dampers in his armor boots.
The curved bottom of the round chamber made movement towards the Executrix slow for most of them, as they struggled from slipping on the smooth deck. But they finally reached it a few minutes later and approached the flickering processing node warily and with weapons withdrawn. The single red eye glowed brightly as it sensed their presence, and a mechanical voice emanated from the sphere. In the vast, lifeless chamber, it seemed puny, weak. 'Assistance required. System maintenance and defense grid has been destroyed by the data virus...'
The voice strongly reminded Cosette of Kel-ten; so blunt and lifeless, yet capable of conveying emotion, even if it was incapable of such a concept. She took a step forward and asked, "What happened? What virus? Was Ontonus IV under attack?"
'A network virus infiltrated and took control of 64% of our ships, despite attempts to isolate it. Our ships turned on us, and would have destroyed us were it not for your arrival. Remainder of ships deactivated to prevent further spreading of virus.'
A Buckaneer inquired, "Then you were not responsible for the recent attacks on the Dylanus Dominion and Central?" The Executrix paused, as if hesitating on whether to answer or not. 'Attack on the Dominion was deliberate, the attack on Central was out of my control. We were retaliating to the Dominion's violation of the peace agreement, and for spreading the network virus.'
Garpenal roughly pushed Cosette aside and advanced, "Lies! The Dominion never unleashed a virus! For all we know, it may have been one of your insidious creations, a weapon in your campaign against us that you lost control of. You deserve this, scheming bastards!"
'I have data files which clearly show their violation of the neutral zone treaty...'
Without warning, Garpenal unsheathed his anti-matter blade in a graceful motion, and plunged it deep into the Executrix's processing node, up to the hilt. The Executrix emitted a piercing, metallic whine, a death-rattle; and the node's glow abruptly disappeared. The vast array of lights and coprocessors along its massive surface died out, and the entire central chamber plunged into utter darkness.
Cosette fumbled with her EVA suit's controls, activating its helmet floodlights, "My God, Garpenal, you killed it! What the hell...?!"
A harsh beam of light pierced through the chamber, catching the Xerusian in mid-charge. Garpenal was about to run her through with his blade when the floodlights blinded him only three steps from her. Cosette reacted, bringing her plasma rifle to bear and squeezing the trigger. The Xerusian's armor absorbed the blast, but was thrown four feet back by the impact. Everyone raised their weapons and aimed it at Garpenal as he quickly returned to a standing position, unfazed.
"I am sorry it has to end this way, Commander Knox," he grimly announced. "But the Dominion cannot afford an exposi as of now. You all know too much and will regretfully have to be eliminated like the useless mass of metal behind me." One of the Buckaneers snarled, and hurled itself at him with outstretched talons. Garpenal raised his other arm, and the three projectiles punched through the assailant. The Buckaneer fell on the chamber in a growing pool of its own black blood.
"No!" Cosette fired at the Xerusian, the shots aimed wildly at the semi-gloom. Kel-ten followed suit, raining him with tachyon beams. Garpenal had deployed the eternium buckler integrated into his left gauntlet, and deftly blocked the beams. He raised his arm again and fired back at the Tecanoid, but Kel-ten's reflex upgrades kicked into action as he dodged them despite his bulk.
Suddenly, the entire chamber shook, trembled, followed by an automated alarm, "Warning, integrity safeguards failing. Chamber collapse imminent." Several conduits on the chamber's cavernous ceiling erupted, raining metal shards on the combatants below.
"How convenient," Garpenal smiled, sheathing his blade. "I couldn't have come up with a better excuse for your deaths. Now if you'll excuse me..."
The remaining Buckaneer detached a small metal pod from his belt, and hurled it at Garpenal just as he began to bend his knees for an accelerated leap to the chamber's top. A flickering aura surrounded the Xerusian, and he fell in a heap of armor to the floor, twitching.
"That was for my brother, you snake!" he hissed, then turned to Cosette. "Commander, we have to get of here. The chamber is about to give way without the Executrix to monitor the entire planet's systems."
"What did you...?" she warily turned towards the fallen Garpenal.
"A disruptor grenade," the Buckaneer replied, stretching his powerful wings. "It has disabled his armor's power, at least for five minutes... Killing him outright would be far too generous."
"Let us go," Kel-ten admonished, "We are out of the Retribution's range to be phase-shifted out. We have to return to the platform above."
Kel-ten and Cosette each took hold of the Buckaneer's arms, and they began their swift ascension to the catwalk above. Garpenal's enraged voice followed their ascent, echoing off the chamber's weakened walls.
"Gertuc! You will regret this, hural! I will hunt you all to ends of the cosmos!"
The trio stepped onto the platform seconds later and slipped out of the chamber in a halo of crimson light, just as the entire room gave way, crushing Garpenal and the Executrix's dead shell beneath tons of metal and machinery.
