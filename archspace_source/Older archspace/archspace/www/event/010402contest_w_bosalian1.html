The Trak'na Chronicles: THE HAMMER FALLS
by Carlo Jan P. Garingarao


Encircling the metallic sphere of Ontonus IV was probably the largest assembly of Trak'na Peace Initiative ships that the star cluster had ever witnessed. More than a hundred separate fleets from Initiative-member councils orbited the Evintos stronghold in a formation of concentric circles, with the Jarnapah Protectorate capital ship, the Retribution, holding stationary orbit in the middle of the armada.

A navigator detached himself from the titanic vessel's psi-drive grid, and projected his thoughts to the Bosalian positioned in the heart of the Retribution's integrity node; Jehrom, Overseer of the Initiative, chief diplomat of the Protectorate, supreme commander of the special assault armada to TCS-289.

'Overseer, the away team has returned... without Teradin Garpenal and Lokul-Ra. Commander Knox and the others are on their way from the phase-shift conduits.'

Jehrom responded to the report with a simple thought of dismissal, troubled at the news. The Human Knox had descended into the Evintos Executrix's central chamber deep within the planet below, along with her Tecanoid right-hand, Garpenal, and two Buckaneers not more than ten minutes ago. If anything had happened to Garpenal, the Xerusian's psi-damping armor would've prevented him from sensing it. All he had detected in the past few minutes was a faint and diffused sensation of alarm and pain... they were too deep below Ontonus IV's steel surface for even his psionic powers to reach, beneath miles of impeding machinery.

But if Knox and her companions were already on board the Retribution... Jehrom scanned the massive vessel for the Human commander of the Autonomy fleet, and instantly found her amidst the sea of Bosalian souls that mulled within the ship. He could strongly feel her thoughts dominated by confusion and anxiety, but refrained from probing deeper into her mind. From the rigid Tecanoid walking at her side, nothing, as expected; from the sole remaining Buckaneer behind her, indignant rage. It was clear something disturbing had happened during their visit to the Executrix.

A section of the dome-shaped command chamber's smooth bulkhead flickered, then vanished, revealing a long wide corridor through which Cosette Knox and company traversed. The slender Human female, still in her EVA suit, had detached her helmet, exposing her troubled features.

"Jehrom, something is going on here, something that involves more than just the Initiative," she started, stopping three paces from the Bosalian as he hovered within the confines of the Retribution's integrity node. "The Executrix mentioned something about a data virus that has recently infiltrated the planet's systems, the defense grid, most of their ships; beyond their ability to isolate and control. It was badly weakened, and blamed the Xerusians for the virus..."

'Garpenal, where is he?' Jehrom interrupted.

Kel-ten, Knox's Tecanoid first officer, answered the question for her with his flat, unemotional voice, "The teradin terminated the Executrix, enraged by its accusation. Then he attempted to terminate all of us as well."

The Buckaneer at Knox's right flexed his taloned hands and hissed between clenched teeth, "He slew Lokul-Ra before we could bring him down! By Heri's claw, he deserved a far worse death that being buried alive in that chamber!"

"He was hiding something," Knox frowned. "Even though he denied the Dominion's involvement with the virus, there was still something he didn't want us to find out."

Jehrom fell silent for a moment, digesting the new turn of events: the Evintos weren't responsible for the attacks on Central and the Dominion, but were devastated by a network virus, while the Dominion denies having spread it. Then exactly who was behind this entire crisis?

That was when the Bosalian felt it. A focused, overpowering, collective thought, intensifying by the second. He could sense it all around them, an invisible sphere that engulfed the entire armada, shrinking. He scanned the navigators in the chamber, but their mental capacities weren't strong enough to feel it.

Cosette noticed the Bosalian's aura flicker for an instant, then darken a slight fraction. "Jehrom? What is it?"

The inquiry was ignored, as Jehrom tried to sort the mind-thoughts that bombarded at him from all directions. It was clouding his focus, preventing him from identifying the source of the emotions. He forced himself from instinctively enclosing his mind with a barrier, and let the sensations filter through him.

Primal conviction... seeming mindless in its single-pointedness... destruction of the natives... glory to the hive... death... devastation... conquest... 'Jarnapah, it is too much!' Jehrom gripped his head between his hands, bending painfully as he detached himself from the flow of thoughts and raised a mental barrier to ward off the deafening rush. The entire ship, linked to his mind, throbbed, its interior luminaries flickering. Cosette and a navigator rushed up the integrity node in alarm.

"What is wrong?" 'We... have to get out of here...' the Overseer managed to project in spite of the mental stress. 'This is all... a trap...' "A trap?"

Cosette lowered her head towards his.

"By whom?"

'The Targoid... they are here...'

"Oh my God," she gasped, unable to grasp what was happening. It just wasn't possible, was it? There wasn't any reports of Targoid infestations in the neighboring star clusters, and they would've noticed their arrival.

Another navigator declared, 'Warning, unidentified vessels approaching the Initiative armada... they're everywhere!'

Less than second later, the Retribution shuddered under the onslaught of weapons fire, its shields fluctuating. Cosette glanced at the control chamber's expansive viewscreen, at a horrifying tableau: countless streaks of gold and green weaving through the Initiative armada faster than her eyes could follow, raining anti-matter missiles on the unsuspecting fleets. Buds of flame blossomed a moment later as unshielded ships detonated, torn apart by the missiles.

We have to get back to the ship," Cosette broke off from her shock, then grabbed Kel-ten's arm. As the two of them raced towards the phase-shift conduits, she activated her wrist-band, "All DMC ships, this is Commander Knox, we are under attack! Engage but do not break formation!"

The Dupre Autonomy's fleet was reduced to half by the time Cosette reached the capital ship's command deck, just after a single assault wave from the Targoids.

"Kel-ten, how many enemy ships are there?"
"Approximately 500,000 targets, commander," the Tecanoid intoned.

Her ops officer announced in alarm, "They're moving too fast for our weapons to lock on! Our ships can barely hit them!"

The deck heaved to port as a missile impacted their failing shields, and the ops console erupted in a shower of sparks and metal shards. The officer was on his back on the deck, groaning in pain, his face a bloody mess.

"Johner, take him to medical!" she barked, then established a link to what little remained of her fleet. "All ships, reroute all power to shields. Our weapons are useless."

The situation was getting out of hand. Not even Jehrom's clever ring formation, which had worked so well against the renagade Evintos armada, could stand against the Targoid wave. If they stayed here much longer...

Jehrom's image flickered in the comm screen, 'All fleet commanders, retreat! Get yourselves out of here as fast as possible and regroup at Central. There is no way we can win this battle.'

Cosette quickly ordered the DMC lancers to initiate hyper-drive jump, but Kel-ten blatantly reported, "Commander, if we jump into hyper-drive, we risk collision with the Targoid vessels... a very high risk."

They were quickly running out of options. Staying and fighting would mean certain death, just as certain as retreating without hyper-drive speed. Each second she hesitated, another DMC ship was destroyed, all the lives within it lost.

'Overseer, the armada cannot retreat, not with the Targoid ships swarming all around us,' the navigator at Jehrom's side urgently announced. 'We have to immobilize them somehow.'

Jehrom, still recovering from his recent psionic exertion, straightened himself and projected to all the control crew, 'Activate the psionic wave pulse emitter... there is nothing more we can do.'

The navigators paused, then hurried about to obey his command with grim resolve. They all gathered in a ring around the integrity node, where a complex circular apparatus was slowly rising from a compartment within the deck. Along the ring were purple, multi-faceted crystals in regular intervals, one in front of each navigator. They all gripped the crystals, then focused all their psionic strength, feeding them into the ring, into the integrity node in which Jehrom stood.

In the hulking Retrubution's exterior, an opening appeared on the topmost deck, directly above the control chamber. A pulsing orb extended from within the ship, coming into full view of the entire armada; the emitter.

'What we do now is the greatest gift we can offer to the Initiative,' Jehrom proclaimed. 'We give them a chance to live and fight, in exchange for ours. If any of you here do not wish to continue, speak now.'

In response, the navigators intensified their projections into the array, until the node glowed with a golden brilliance, engulfing Jehrom with a blinding halo. It was all he needed to know...

The emitter was filled with an intense blue light, rapidly increasing in brightness. It overflowed an instant later, issuing forth from in a roiling wave, spreading through the armada and the Targoid ships, engulfing everything in a radiant cloud.

Their mental connections temporarily severed by the wave, the Targoid ships came to an abrupt halt without a central thought to guide their collective.

"Commander, the Targoid ships have stopped," Kel-ten reported. "The Retribution has disabled them with a massive psionic pulse."

Instantly, Cosette realized what this meant for Jehrom and his crew. By diverting all their energies to the emitter, they were too exhausted to navigate the massive ship to safety. She didn't intend to let his sacrifice go to waste.

"All ships, hyper-drive jump to Central now!"

Simultaneously, the DMC lancers disappeared in halos of light, the surviving Initiative ships followed suit.

'The armada has escaped, Overseer,' the navigator's mental voice was barely perceptible. They were all spent, their powers drained by activating the emitter and disabling the Targoid.

Jehrom, his psionic aura all but gone, managed to turn to the viewscreen. The Targoid ships were slowly re-establishing their mental links, and slowly surrounded the defenseless Retribution in a dense swarm.
