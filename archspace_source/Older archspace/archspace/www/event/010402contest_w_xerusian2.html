Hunter, Hunted:Games of War
by Erich Devendorf

The dull glow of artificial light surrounded the primary engine compartment, as the drip of condensation echoed in the otherwise eerily silent chamber.  A lone Xerusian warrior standing two and a half meters tall was present with a sword of light illuminating as she stalked the
compartment in search of her prey.  Above the slightly neurotic drip of water, the inevitable clink and clatter of full battle gear, glinting in the light, could be heard with each deliberate step taken.  The bowels of a spacecraft was no place for the weak of heart, but that seldom
presented a problem for a race born and bred to battle.  This endeavor was no less a battle than the Great Wars of the Samvarra system, fighting the fanatical Xeloss. No, it was no less a battle, only the pursuit of a spy was a slightly more subtle hunt.

    An echo resounded through the chamber as the prey slipped and slid around a corner.  Damnable spies thought the Xerusian as she continued following the source of the sound with cautious steps.  There was no greater threat than an enemy cornered as it had nothing to lose. The warrior knew that much in spite of her relative lack of experience. With a whisper she reported into main security

 "We have a runner, unknown faction, unknown race and origin" she said, only to be greeted by the main computer.

 "Acknowledged 21004, transferring you to Sector Security Chief." replied the emotionless metallic voice.

 "Damn machine," she whispered under her breath. If there was one thing she hated, it was being referred to as a number.  She continued stalking her prey, sword held outward with its tip just below the horned helm which covered her face and attached to the main suit of armor.  Her
armor was capable of repelling the best infantry weapons, so she wasn't necessarily scared of attack as much as her quarry escaping.  A click echoed in her helmet as the Sector Security Chief asked for an update and she repeated her response to the main computer.  She was then
referred to the Local Security Advisor, which meant she would have to go through the litany once again before she reached her own "Personal Supervisor."  The only thing which seemed to bother her more than being referred to as a number was probably the  inefficient workings of the Xerusian bureaucracy.  You would think a military culture could have managed to gain an edge in the efficiency department somewhere along the lines, but tradition dictated things be
done a particular way.  Who was she to argue with tradition?

 Still fuming over Xerusian Society as a whole which, although it had a few flaws, was obviously superior to all others, she almost failed to notice her prey had ceased making any sounds.  The spy, whoever it was, was planning something she knew and her military training kicked in at
just the right second to allow her to deflect a plasma rifle's beam with her shield.  Nonetheless the weapon left a deep  score on the ships hull which would have to be repaired, probably by
her for allowing it to happen.  Then again, she would be damned if she was going to take a plasma rifle in the chest for no reason.

 She suddenly realized that her pursuit had taken her to the entrance of a new room, and she carefully studied her surroundings from her hiding position next to the airlock.  The chamber was dimly lit, which always seemed to be the case, with a single catwalk spanning a nearly 100 meter gap to the rooms exit, a door which read E-7-G on it.  Off to either side, the chamber fell into nothingness, and the ceiling was so high that it might as well be considered the sky on a planet.  Somehow the spies always seemed to find rooms like these to make their last stand.
Just in time, for once, the Local Security Advisor buzzed in and she updated him as to her situation, requesting the far door, E-7-G be locked shut.  This, naturally, would have to take a number of bureaucratic steps to be completed, but in around twenty minutes it should have been accomplished.  Now, she only had to watch and wait for her chance to move.

  She considered calling for reinforcements, but honor dictated she handle it alone.  To request aid was a sign of weakness.  After sufficient time had passed she readied herself to enter the chamber, realizing confirmation of her request to shut the door might very well take days.

 There were a number of approaches which might be taken to enter the chamber, but it seemed that direct methods usually worked best for a Xerusian.  She readied her sword and shield for the soon to begin conflict as she contemplated her choice of arms.  Of course, in a modern
age the classic Xerusian weapons of shield and sword may seem out of date until one realizes a single key fact: it is much more  likely that stray shots from conventional weapons will destroy
something important, and possibly depressurize a section of a spacecraft, than a sword and shield.  Now, projectiles and beams may work to eliminate an enemy, but the Xerusians also tend to have a strong sense of self preservation so swords and shields it was.

 With weapons drawn she silently, except for the damnable clinking of her armor, advanced into the chamber from her hiding spot.  The imposter had to be in here somewhere she knew, but doubts began to surface as she nearly reached the end of the catwalk.  Maybe the spy had escaped out the exit she thought...

 It was then that the devastating psychic strike rippled through her consciousness, with the only visible sign of the struggle her slight swaying on the catwalk and lowering of weapons.  Images flashed through her mind, each in grotesque detail, of the red robed Xeloss priests performing ghastly rituals over the defeated corpses of their enemies. More horrifying than this was the fact that on each tortured body  she could see only her face, screaming in agony.  Fear gripped her
heart and she wished to flee away from her obviously superior foe.  She stood up shaking, searching for an exit, but the way ahead of her was blocked by the immense security door. It was when she turned around that her nightmare became reality.

 Standing nearly three meters in height was the imposing figure of a Xeloss warrior priest.  His red robes moved slightly in the chamber breeze and a deep crimson glow surrounded his hands where he held a small crystal, the focus of his psychic attack.  A cruel grin spread across a white skinned face, dominated by various tentacle like appendages, as the priest enjoyed the destruction he wrought upon the mind of his stalker.  There was no escape for her thought the priest, she was trapped...

 While she cringed in fear of her towering enemy the Xerusian warrior was suddenly gripped by a strange desperation and a bloodthirsty urge to strike back at her tormentor.  In an unexpected motion she stood and uttered a primitive cry, focusing all her fear and hatred into that sound as her instructors had taught her a few short months ago.  The crimson glow of the Xeloss crystal receded, and a blinding white  light exploded from the heart of her armor.  Lightning fast she shot forward to her startled opponent, but he was not to be caught entirely unawares.

 As fast as the glowing white blade struck downward at the head of the priest another crimson blade came to meet it, with sheer chance deflecting his second scimitar off of her shield.  Feeling the impact she jumped backwards, and a for a short second silence dominated the chamber as the two enemies stared one another down in anticipation of the true battle about to erupt.
Sparks of crimson and white filled the chamber as the twin scimitars of the Xeloss met the
 sword and shield of the Xerusians.  Stroke followed counter stroke in a brilliant display of martial prowess, but the weight of battle armor began to take its toll upon the Xerusian.  Her stokes gradually slowed and a new panic gripped her as she was pressed increasingly on the
defensive.  Play to her strengths she thought, and realized the burden of the armor was also an asset.  With a quick prayer to whatever god of battle which might be watching she dropped her shield, gripping the white sword in two hands.  In response two red scimitars darted towards
her helm, and the shock of the impact nearly jarred her off the catwalk.

 However, she ignored the pain, instead striking with an overhead smash as the scimitars remained embedded in her thick armor.  Her blow landed true, the Xeloss priest crumpled, and the red of his scimitars and the crystal now hanging around his neck went out.

 With a wrench she removed the helm, now decorated by twin scimitars, from her head and studied the body of the priest, deciding she liked them much more when they were dead.  Quickly, she rummaged among his robes and found what she had been seeking.  A token attached to his robe indicating he was among the "Dead Ones" of Xeloss society, an elite
group who were never to return to their race and lived only to bring destruction to their rivals.  Another suicide mission she thought as she heard the buzz of her communicator within the helm.  Quickly she put it on and was greeted to the clicking of locks behind her and an automated voice which said:

"Request processed 21004, securing airlock E-7-G."
