The Trak'na Chronicles: THE BASTION
By Carlo Jan P. Garingarao

The call came suddenly and without warning, breaking the centuries-long monotony the children had long slipped into since the horde arrived in Trak'na. For years innumerable, they had all drifted together aimlessly, half-aware and half-asleep, watching silently as the stars and planets floated past them, as strange metal objects scurried around them in their own little ventures. They were now in unfamiliar space, hundreds of light years away from home. In a time long forgotten, during the golden years of the Empire, the peace they had enjoyed was shattered by the dreaded Imperial Armada. Half of their kith had perished in the assault, and the mother severely wounded. It was a miracle how they had managed to escape from the formidable Armada, and ended up in the star cluster now known as Trak'na for the mother to recuperate for all those years.
Now it was time to rise, for their purpose to be rekindled. Their mother was whole once more and was gently, yet clearly beckoning them from the twilight state of hibernation. One by one, the offspring responded to her summons.

The call came suddenly and without warning, breaking the deep, dreamless slumber Cosette Knox had slipped into. The comm-band around her left wrist was bleating persistently, signalling an incoming transmission. She groggily rose from her bunk, and stretched her aching neck muscles while tapping the respond button.
"Knox here."
"Sorry for the wake-up call, commander," a familiar male voice replied. It was Vernon Wallace, her chief of security. Despite the officer's trademark nonchalance, she could detect the faintest hint of concern in his voice. "Kel-ten has picked up something unusual in his sensors, you better come up here and have a look for yourself." Cosette was just starting to emerge from post-sleep fog, and slowly trudged to the lavatory. "Sure you can't handle it, Vernie? It's barely past midnight..."
"Well, if this means anything to you, it's the Agerus. They're on the move."

As far as Lieutenant Commander Vernon Wallace of the Dupre Marine Corps was concerned, a security detail on Firma was the most uneventful assignment one could ever end up with. Even monotonous, half-hour patrols on the Dupre Autonomy's rarely-threatened borders had to be less boring than keeping watch on a system-wide mining operation. Everyone on this bloody station kept telling themselves how "important" Firma was to the Autonomy, as it provided almost all of the resources to its twelve planets. Not that Vernie thought it wasn't. Well, at least that kept almost everyone's mind off how dreadfully boring the system was.
Until today, of course.
Vernie brushed back his long, red hair that kept getting into his eyes as he reclined on the command post, his booted feet propped up on the nearest console's rim. At twenty-one Human years of age, he was probably the youngest marine in the Corps to garner such a rank; not that it mattered much. A six-year old could easily perform his duties: sit down and keep watch. "How are they, Kel-ten?" he asked the Tecanoid behind the sensor console to his left, without as much as taking his eyes off the bridge's ceiling. "Still ornery?"
The cybernetic officer dutifully read the readout in front of him, and swivelled his head to bluntly reply, "The Agerus life-forms have compressed around a site on the system's borders, and have stopped. As for your second inquiry, the station's sensors cannot ascertain their emotions. Perhaps if we acquire the services of a Bosalian..." "Thank you, that will be all for now, Kel-ten," Vernie grinned. When the Corps assigned the Tecanoid as part of the station's senior crew three months ago, he had found the cyborg's lack of emotion and literal line of thought quite amusing.
The bridge's doors slid open just as Vernie was about to check his chronometer for the third time, letting in an attractive, slender Human female with delicate features, black hair kept in a high ponytail; Cosette Knox. Even though she five years his senior, Vernie couldn't resist ogling at her any chance he got. Perhaps the only setback was that Cosette had the mind of a forty-year old. "What's the situation?"
Vernie quickly scooted off the command chair, and reported as she slipped into it, "The Agerus were calculated to pass by Firma's borders two hours ago, but somehow, they changed course. Now they've completely stopped, and seem to be gathering at the edge of the system."
"This is the first time in recorded local history that they have deviated from their normal behavior," Kel-ten added with his flat voice. "Ever since they arrived in Trak'na six hundred fifty-eight years ago, all they have been observed to do was float from one corner of the star cluster to another."
Cosette was well aware of the fact that this local Agerus swarm had never been considered an active threat, except perhaps as a hindrance to space lanes. It had been reported passing through the Jarnapah Protectorate last year, past the Dylanus Dominion half a year ago; even past the Peace Initiative Headquarters, embodiment of the peace agreement itself, without as much as an alarm.
"Do we have a visual?"
The Tecanoid started working on his console, "Linking to outpost 32. Displaying..."
The large screen at the bridge's front blinked, showing the vast emptiness of space. To the lower-right corner spun Firma VI, a small gas planet around which the unmanned outpost revolved. A small, distant brown cloud could be seen in the middle of the screen.
"Magnify by a factor of ten," Cosette slightly leaned forward in her chair, squinting. At once, the view zoomed in, revealing an eerie sight: Agerus planetary life-forms, gathered in a dense cloud. They were all completely still, yet Cosette could not help but sense dread. They seemed to be communing with one another; a mute collective planning its next course of action.
"Keep all our standby ships on yellow alert," the commander finally ordered. "The moment they cross into the system, notify me at once."
"Should we inform the mining colonies, ma'am?" Vernie asked.
"No... not yet. We don't want to cause any panic. It's our job to keep these miners secure, and so far, there's no reason to alarm them."
Vernie frowned, expecting things to heat up. "I guess this show's over then."
"Let's hope so," Cosette sighed, leaned back, and pressed her palms to her eyelids. "I know how damn boring this post is, Vernon. But it's better than a full-fledged invasion. I'll be in my quarters if you need me."
She rose and started for the doors, when Kel-ten's loud yet calm voice announced, "Commander, they are moving again... on a direct course for Firma VI."
"How long?" she quickly returned to her post.
"Twenty minutes. They are moving at an accelerated rate."
"Scramble all standby ships to intercept at once. Vernon, you're in command of the fleet. We're not sure what they're up to, so don't fire unless they make the first move. They may just be passing through."
Vernie shot up like a spring, trying to hide a grin of delight. "Aye, aye, ma'am."
Cosette turned to a communications officer to her right, "Notify DMC HQ."

A total of fifty corvettes and three cruisers had formed a barricade before Firma VI by the time the Agerus came into firing range. It wasn't much, Vernie knew, but it was the best they had in the system. The heavier DMC ships were stationed on the Autonomy's borders and around the capital planet, more than three hours away, and Cosette wasn't calling for back-up unless they were certain the Agerus were taking the offensive.
The corvettes were in spread in an even line, with Vernie's ship in the middle, and the larger cruisers close behind. It wasn't usual for the fleet commander to be in a smaller ship, but Vernie liked working with a small crew; easy to manoeuvre, easy to manage.
"The targets are in weapons range, sir," his female conn-officer announced.
"Power up the gatling rail guns," he replied, then switched on the fleet-wide comm, "All ships, weapons ready. Fire only in my command."
The life-forms were approaching at a relatively slow yet alarming speed. Sensors had reported a total of forty-nine separate bodies, the smallest half a kilometer in length. The swarm advanced in a straight line, straight for the barricade itself.
"Steady," Vernie commanded, more to himself than to the entire fleet, as the Agerus closed the distance. "Steady..."
Suddenly, the swarm came to a dead halt. Without warning, a tiny round object streaked from the middle of the Agerus mass, sped past the barricade, and vanished into the roiling atmosphere of the gaseous planet.
"What the hell was that?" Vernie swiveled to the conn.
"I'm not sure, sir... an organic mass, encapsulated by a thick crystalline shell."
Things came to a standstill for the next two minutes, both fleets facing one another. The conn officer suddenly warned, "Lieutenant Commander, something's wrong. Planetary activity has gone haywire! Liquid mantle is solidifying at an unbelievable rate, atmospheric gas declining... Oh my God, somehow they're transforming the entire planet!"
"Any personnel?"
"None, siphon tankers periodically gather gas from the planet, but no permanent facilities have been built."
Two more of the objects flew past them and dove into Firma VI, then three more...
"I'll be damned if this isn't an act of war," Vernie gritted his teeth. "All ships, attack!"
The corvettes opened fire, showering the Agerus fleet with rail gun projectiles. Small chunks of organic crystal flew from the bodies, but they were barely harmed. A wave of death spores came in response.
"Evasive maneuvers!"
Two corvettes weren't as fortunate as the rest, and were ripped apart by the explosive spores. The trio of cruisers slowly advanced, and unleashed a barrage of tachyon beams. But the Agerus merely absorbed them, and retaliated with another wave of spores.
"Corvettes, switch to fusion missiles!" Vernon shouted at the comm. "Beam and projectile weapons are useless!"
Three of the meteor-like targets shattered to pieces as the missiles hit their targets, but it took too many missiles, and the corvettes could carry only a handful of them in any one time. To make matters worse, despite the firepower the cruisers packed, they weren't outfitted with missiles.
"All ships, fall back once your missiles are depleted," Vernon ordered, his own ship already out of ordnance. "Regroup at Firma III as soon as you are reloaded. We won't win this way..." Swiftly, the fleet retreated, as the Agerus horde began to encircle the abandoned Firma VI.

The DMC fleet had lost nine corvettes in the failed defence, and was now orbiting high above the mining colonies of Firma III. Already, the evacuation transports were leaving the planet in a steady stream, bearing miners and their families alike to safety. Commander Knox's corvette had joined them less than an hour earlier, and was holding a briefing aboard one of the cruisers.
"All right, the Agerus have left Firma VI two minutes ago and are headed for this planet," Cosette planted her hands on the rim of the holo-sim in the middle of the packed conference room. "We have about half an hour before they arrive, so we better act fast."
Vernon was standing close to the commander, lips curving slightly in a faint smile. Perhaps thinking like a forty-year old did have its advantages. The room was packed with senior officers, almost all with the same grim look on their faces; except for the Tecanoid ones, of course. The briefing was over in five minutes, with a desperate plan that was their only hope of saving the entire system.
"Remember your assignments, men," the commander switched off the holo-sim and straightening herself. "The DMC reinforcements won't arrive for two more hours, we're the only hope for Firma. Dismissed."
Kel-ten stayed behind along with Vernon and Cosette as the other officers quickly returned to their ships. "You are aware, commander, that our plan has a very small probability of succeeding."
"Yes," she replied, sighing in anxiety, switching the sim back on to run their plan one more time. "All too aware. But it's the best we have."
"Have you considered a retreat?" the cyborg continued indifferently.
"That is not an option. We don't have enough time to evacuate all of the system's colonies before they arrive. This has to stop here, no further."

The Agerus horde came as expected, plodding steadily towards Firma III. At the commander's signal, ten corvettes detached from the fleet, and flew straight for the swarm.
"Team Alpha, aim for the center of the swarm, two volleys," Cosette's voice filtered through the ships' comms. "Kel-ten, ready with your sensors. We only have one shot at this.
" Ten missiles streaked for the heart of the gathering, and impacted with the front-line Agerus entities and destroying two. The ships swung around, and released another wave of missiles. This time, the horde retaliated, but the corvettes had quickly retreated, returning to the main fleet.
Cosette peered over Kel-ten's shoulder at his sensor readout, "Well, have you found her?"
"Note how the Agerus in the front reacted to the first wave of missiles," the Tecanoid explained, pointing at the display. "They slightly massed towards a certain point, as if shielding something. I cannot pinpoint exactly where she is, but based on data from the two waves, I have calculated four possible mother bodies.
" His metallic finger tapped at four of the Agerus entities on the screen, "I'm relaying this information to the Team Bravo." "Bravo Leader here," Vernon's voice came through the comm seconds later. "Commander, with four possible targets, it's not going to be easy. Are you sure destroying her will disable the entire fleet?"
"Not sure, Vernie," she replied. "The Agerus have never been that well researched. But we don't have enough firepower to take all of them out. We're counting on you."
"Two minutes before they arrive, commander," Kel-ten reminded her.
She switched over to the entire fleet, "Initiate offensive at my mark..."
Cosette stared at the swarm in the main screen as it approached, turned to Kel-ten's blank, passive gaze, then took a deep breath. "Now!"
The entire DMC fleet advanced, meeting the horde a minute from reaching the planet. Fusion missiles, tachyon beams, and death spores flew between the two fleets in a dizzying display of firepower. Sixteen corvettes and only five Agerus detonated in the next five minutes, and the DMC fleet was slowly losing its ground. The Agerus has compressed at the front, forming a dense, impenetrable wall.
"Team Bravo, now!"
Preoccupied with defending the mother body, the Agerus had failed to notice a small group of corvettes that had swung completely behind them as soon as the battle begun: Team Bravo, ten corvettes led by Vernon Wallace. The ships split into two groups of five, and concentrated on two of the rearmost entities, pounding them with fusion missiles, completely obliterating them into dust.

"Team Bravo has destroyed two of the possible targets," Kel-ten announced, able to concentrate on his sensors and the ship's weapons at the same time. "No effect."
"That leaves two more," Cosette leaned towards the comm. "Vernie, take them out as quick as you can. One more swing..." Their corvette buckled as a death spore impacted at her starboard shields, the ship's running lights flickering for a second. One of the cruisers' shields had collapsed, wreathed in flame as death spores punched right through its failing defenses and its hull. The fleet was not going to hold back the tide much longer...

"All right, team," Vernon took a deep breath as they swung around in a tight curve, "one last try. Let's fry these babies!" A terrifying sight met them as they completed their turn: the Agerus front line had broken up, and was slowly shifting to the rear to protect the mother body. A large cloud of spores was racing straight for them in retaliation. Six corvettes burst, there was barely time to react. That left four, with enough missiles to destroy only one Agerus entity. "We only have a fifteen-second window to target, sir," Vernon's conn officer warned, voice laced with alarm. Time was running out, two targets, and ammo for only one. Vernon closed his eyes, mumbled a prayer, and squared his shoulders in firm resolve. "Converge on target three and fire all weapons."
The missiles arced, and shattered another Agerus into crystal shards. Another spore barrage engulfed them a split-second later, wiping out the entire Bravo Team.

"The entire team has been destroyed," the Tecanoid announced in a flat tone, untouched by the fact. "The horde still seems to be unaffacted."
"Damn it!" Cosette cursed, slamming her fist on the command console's rim. "Retreat, all ships retreat!"
What little of the DMC fleet remained ceased fire, turned around and leapt into full speed. They had failed, Firma was lost. By the time the reinforcements arrived, it would be too late. "Commander, the Agerus... they have ceased all activity. The mother body has been destroyed."
Cosette rushed over to his console and blinked, unable to believe her eyes. The horde had completely stopped and the sacrifice of Vernon and his men had saved the entire system. Their lives had not been lost in vain after all. A grin crept across her face, and she threw her arms around Kel-ten and hugged him with all her might. The Tecanoid merely turned his head and stared at her in indifference.
"Call off the retreat, Kel-ten. We won."
